Susan and John Letby denounced a new Netflix documentary as an invasion of privacy and said they will not watch it. The Investigation Of Lucy Letby is due to be released globally on Wednesday February 4; the couple said the film uses footage shot in their home without their knowledge.

We will not watch it. It would likely kill us if we did.
— Susan and John Letby

They accused Detective Superintendent Paul Hughes of appearing to hold a deep grudge against them and questioned why he had been allowed to show footage from their home. The couple also warned the film could turn their house into a tourist attraction.

Trailer footage

The trailer includes footage not previously released: officers entering the Letby family home; bodycam clips showing Letby sitting up in bed and being told her rights while still in her pyjamas; and later scenes of her being led from the house in a dressing gown, with a voice off camera telling someone not to look.

At her trial, Letby said she had been taken to a police station while wearing pyjamas. Prosecutors disputed that and said the detail had been invented to gain sympathy.

Police and prosecutors

Cheshire Constabulary said it passed material to prosecutors last year linked to eight possible additional attempted murders and one alleged murder at the Countess of Chester Hospital. The Crown Prosecution Service said the evidential test was not met in any of those cases and that Letby would face no further charges, a result the force publicly criticised as not the outcome it had anticipated throughout its investigation.

The disagreement between the force and the CPS is a central factual tension: Cheshire Constabulary says it sent material for prosecutors to review, while the CPS concluded the evidential threshold was not met and declined to bring further charges.

Legal status and campaigners

Letby was twice denied permission to appeal in 2024, and a campaign group has submitted reports to the Criminal Cases Review Commission seeking to have her convictions overturned.

John Letby said he first went to Blacon police station in March 2017 to report that consultants Stephen Brearey and Ravi Jayaram were making Lucy a scapegoat.

Commentary and speculation

Nadine Dorries, in the Daily Mail, described the footage as salacious and degrading and suggested the police may be trying to bolster the conviction. She wrote that payments to expert witnesses for Dr Dewi Evans could run into seven figures, and said Cheshire Police opened a bidding process in which at least six production companies competed for access, a contest won by ITN.

The Daily Mail reported mounting doubts among eminent experts about the safety of the prosecution case, and MP David Davis told Times Radio the original trial was an exercise in confirmatory bias.

Dame Esther Rantzen told The Sunday Times the case should be re-examined and insisted the pyjama arrest claim was true.

Documentary material and access

Producers and press reports say the trailer includes footage that has never been released publicly, along with interviews with police and lawyers.

Where things stand

Letby is serving 15 whole-life orders for the murders of seven infants and the attempted murders of seven others between June 2015 and June 2016. Cheshire Constabulary says it passed further evidence to prosecutors, but the Crown Prosecution Service concluded the evidential test was not met and no new charges will be brought. Campaigners have submitted reports to the Criminal Cases Review Commission, and Letby was twice denied permission to appeal in 2024. The documentary's global release on Wednesday February 4 and the publication of previously unreleased footage have sharpened competing claims about her arrest and the safety of the prosecution case; the next developments to watch are any CCRC response and the film's release.

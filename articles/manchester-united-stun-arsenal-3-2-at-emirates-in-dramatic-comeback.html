Manchester United stun Arsenal with dramatic 3-2 win after Matheus Cunha's late long-range strike secured a comeback at the Emirates Stadium. The defeat ended Arsenal's 13-game unbeaten run in all competitions and broke an 18-match home unbeaten sequence, while it was United's first league success at the Emirates since December 2017.

Match timeline

Arsenal took the lead in the 29th minute when Lisandro Martinez's attempted clearance ended up in his own net. Later in the first half Martin Zubimendi produced an under-hit backpass that Bryan Mbeumo intercepted, rounded David Raya and fired home to make it 1-1 before half-time.

The finish also represented Bryan Mbeumo's ninth goal in all competitions since joining from Brentford.

Five minutes after the break United regained the lead when Bruno Fernandes picked out Patrick Dorgu, who took a touch and lashed a long-range strike into the top corner; VAR checked for a possible handball but the goal stood.

Arsenal fought back and Mikel Merino bundled home from a corner in the 84th minute to make it 2-2, but three minutes from full-time Bruno Fernandes and Kobbie Mainoo combined to set up Matheus Cunha, who produced a superb 25-metre drive that flashed past David Raya to seal United's victory.

The result leaves Arsenal four points clear at the top with Manchester City and Aston Villa closing the gap.

Managerial question and pundit reaction

Michael Carrick began his interim spell with a surprise 2-0 triumph over Manchester City and has now followed that by beating Arsenal.

Danny Murphy (BBC - Front) said United's big players are turning up, the formation suits them and Michael Carrick deserves credit, and he expects Arsenal to respond positively. Wayne Rooney (BBC - Sport) described Carrick as very capable of being Manchester United's permanent manager, and the Daily Star wrote that Carrick must be considered for the permanent Manchester United manager role.

Al Jazeera suggested Arsenal now face a test of their nerve in the coming weeks, while the Press Association told TheJournal.ie that the defeat could cast doubt on Arsenal's hopes of ending a 22-year title wait. The Daily Star speculated that if Carrick's form continues it may become impossible to ignore him. Danny Murphy said Everyone is going to say, 'are they going to bottle it?'. But I don't think they will.

Off the field, The Sun reported David Beckham posted 'You ok Romeo?' on Instagram and Romeo replied with a screenshot saying 'Don't get too gassed. Mind the gap.'

M5 motorway closed both ways during ongoing police incident causing long delays. Avon and Somerset Police said the stretch between Junction 18 (M49) and Junction 19 (Portishead/Gordano Services) has been shut while officers respond to what the force called a welfare concern. The closure quickly left traffic stationary or heavily slowed on the approaches to the junctions, with drivers reporting long queues and little movement. National Highways warned drivers to expect knock-on travel disruption as the incident was dealt with.

Where and when

All traffic is being temporarily held and stationary traffic due to a police incident on the M5 in both directions from J19 A369 Martcombe Road (Portishead) to J18 M49.
— Inrix

Inrix said the hold was in place just prior to 12:15pm. The traffic service described all traffic as being temporarily held and stationary between J19 A369 Martcombe Road (Portishead) and J18 M49.

Delays and advice

90 min delays northbound and 60 min delays southbound.
— National Highways

National Highways' figures indicated drivers could face extended waits on both carriageways, with official estimates pointing towards the 90/60 minute range. That timeframe, the agency suggested, represented the likely upper end of delays for motorists caught in the queues. It urged motorists to consider taking alternative routes as queues continued to pile up in both directions, and warned that delays would ripple onto local roads as drivers tried to find detours.

Traffic on the ground

Motorway camera feeds showed queues of cars stretching back along the carriageway, images that underlined how the shutdown had halted flow. Visual feeds captured long lines of stopped or creeping traffic and made clear that blockages extended well beyond the immediate closure point.

There was heavy traffic congestion in Bristol city centre and surrounding areas, as well as parts of North Somerset and South Gloucestershire, and some bus services were experiencing delays. Local services and routes were feeling the effect as drivers diverted away from the motorway, putting extra pressure on urban streets and public-transport timetables.

I’ve no idea how far back we are, but we have been moving small distances for a while.
— Motorist (social media)

That personal account matched other social-media posts describing trapped traffic that only inched forward at times and instances where police turned vehicles around to relieve the backlog. Short, repeated movements and frustrated updates were still appearing on feeds shortly after 4.30pm, reflecting the slow progress drivers were experiencing.

Speculation and airport concerns

The Sun speculated the closure could have severe effects on traffic heading towards Bristol Airport, a scenario raised as passengers and commuters considered alternative routes. That projection was presented as a forecast rather than an official finding, and should be read as speculative commentary from a news outlet.

Separately, a social-media user suggested the motorway might reopen at 2:30, a publicly posted estimate rather than an official timetable. That timing was not confirmed by emergency services or highway authorities and should be treated as an informal projection.

Advice and next steps

National Highways urged drivers to consider taking alternative routes as queues continued to pile up in both directions. The guidance from the highways body is an official advisory aimed at reducing pressure on the closed stretch and limiting the wider network impact while police deal with the incident.

State of play

Avon and Somerset Police have confirmed the M5 is closed between Junction 18 (M49) and Junction 19 while officers respond to a welfare concern. National Highways has warned of delays of up to 90 minutes northbound and 60 minutes southbound. The Sun has speculated this could affect journeys to Bristol Airport and a social-media user estimated a 2:30 reopening; both points remain unconfirmed while police continue work and drivers are advised to expect delays and consider alternative routes.

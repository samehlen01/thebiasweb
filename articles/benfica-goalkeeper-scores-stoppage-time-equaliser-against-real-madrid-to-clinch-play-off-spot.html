Benfica goalkeeper scores stoppage-time equaliser against Real Madrid to send Benfica into play-offs. Anatoliy Trubin scored in the 98th minute to give Benfica a 4-2 win over Real Madrid and secure Benfica a place in the Champions League play-offs on goal difference. Some outlets recorded the strike in the 98th minute while the Daily Mail's report listed it in the 97th minute.

Match summary

Andreas Schjelderup scored twice and Vangelis Pavlidis converted a penalty, while Kylian Mbappé scored twice for Real Madrid.

4-2
Benfica 4 (Schjelderup 34, 54; Pavlidis 45+5-pen; Trubin 90+8) — Real Madrid 2 (Mbappé 30, 58)
Final score and scorers

Anatoliy Trubin became the fifth goalkeeper to score in the Champions League.

I am not used to scoring, so for me it was something completely new. I am 24 years old and for me it's the first time.
— Anatoliy Trubin, Benfica goalkeeper

Trubin was forced to leave Donetsk in 2014 and was displaced again in 2022, escaping to Lutsk with his mother and sister.

Benfica players, substitutes and staff invaded the pitch in wild celebration after Trubin's goal, and a Benfica coach slipped and appeared to accidentally injure one of his own players while celebrating.

Consequences and standings

Real Madrid finished ninth in the league phase and must contest the Champions League play-off round rather than qualifying directly for the last 16, which means they will play two more playoff games in February and could face Benfica or Bodø/Glimt.

Trubin's late goal meant Marseille slipped out of the playoff places after their 3-0 defeat to Club Brugge.

Madrid finished the match with nine men after Raúl Asencio and Rodrygo were sent off.

Reactions and analysis

Reactions ranged from regret to admiration. Kylian Mbappé said, "We deserve to be in this situation today. Benfica were better." Jose Mourinho said, "A fantastic goal, a historic goal, a goal that nearly brought the whole stadium down - and I think it was very deserved for us." BBC pundits Stephen Warnock, Nedum Onuoha and Julien Laurens described it as "It's absolutely brilliant!" Julien Laurens wrote, "It's massive for Mourinho, because everything hasn't really worked out for him since taking over." The Daily Mail wrote that "His crowning moment had all the ingredients: drama, consequence, ecstasy."

Speculative takes were clearly signalled as speculation: the BBC suggested "There is a 50% chance Benfica and Real Madrid will meet in the play-off round.", the Daily Mail suggested "Most goalkeepers who score are unlikely to score again.", and the BBC added "Few would rule out Mourinho masterminding something special at the Bernabeu or San Siro if Benfica draw either Real or Inter."

What happens next

Multiple sources forecast that Real Madrid will play two more play-off games in February as they try to reach the last 16, and Benfica's spot in the play-offs was secured by Trubin's late goal.

State of play

Reporting agrees that Trubin's stoppage-time strike decided the match and sent Benfica into the play-offs, that Real Madrid now face two play-off ties in February, and that outlets differ on whether the goal came in the 97th or 98th minute; commentators and managers offered praise, analysis and speculative scenarios about rematches and the rarity of goalkeepers scoring, and the next concrete step is the February play-off schedule.

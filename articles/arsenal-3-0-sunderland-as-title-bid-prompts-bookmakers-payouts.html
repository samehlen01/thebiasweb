Arsenal beat Sunderland 3-0. Arsenal sit nine points clear at the top of the Premier League.

How the game unfolded

Martin Zubimendi opened the scoring with a long-range drive just before half-time. Viktor Gyokeres added a brace, his second and third in the match. He has scored six goals in his last eight games and is the club's top scorer with 13 in all competitions this season. Kai Havertz returned from injury and, operating in midfield, created Gyokeres' first goal. The win was Arsenal's fourth consecutive in all competitions. Leandro Trossard was forced off through injury late in the game. Arsenal remain active across cup competitions, including the League Cup final, the FA Cup and the Champions League last 16.

Reactions and analysis

Rice is really special
— Noah Sadiki, Sunderland midfielder

Paul Merson said Arteta cannot settle on a first-choice striker and that substitutes have often provided the decisive impact. Merson also described Declan Rice as outstanding and consistently high-performing for Arsenal. He said Gabriel Jesus had a difficult hour and pinpointed rotation up front as an unresolved issue. Merson praised Kai Havertz's calmness on the ball and said he created Gyokeres' first goal. Alan Shearer called a 'piece of brilliance' plus effective substitutions the key to breaking down Sunderland's defence. Mikel Arteta said he was 'very, very happy' with the win but warned there are many more games to win. TheJournal.ie wrote that after three straight second-place finishes it appears a matter of when, not if, Arsenal will be crowned champions this season.

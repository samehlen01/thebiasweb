The 2026 Winter Olympics opened in Milano Cortina on Feb. 6, with the official opening ceremony at San Siro stadium in Milan, and organisers said the Games will run through Feb. 22 with a closing ceremony in Verona. The San Siro staging blended spectacle and headline performers as athletes prepared for competition. Organisers said some sports were scheduled to begin in the days before the ceremony to keep the packed programme on track. Those early events included mixed-doubles curling and early ice-hockey preliminaries.

Ceremonies and relay across Italy

Milano Cortina 2026 spans multiple cities across northern Italy, a layout organisers and commentators have described as the most geographically dispersed Winter Olympics. In addition to Milan, athletes are scheduled to parade in Cortina, Livigno and Predazzo, with events clustered by geography and sport to try to limit unnecessary transfers. The opening format deliberately layers simultaneous elements in different towns so that athletes who compete the following day can still take part in local parades. Analysts say that arrangement will complicate travel and logistics for athletes, media and fans, creating heavier demands on transport planning across a stretched calendar. Creative leads have even speculated that the multi-site ceremony could become a model future hosts attempt to copy.

Hundreds of competitors from dozens of countries will be based at more than two dozen venues across northern Italy; Team USA's delegation numbers 232 athletes, according to organiser tallies. Erin Jackson and Frank Del Duca were selected as the U.S. flag bearers by a vote of their fellow athletes and were announced by the U.S. Olympic & Paralympic Committee, a choice intended to reflect athlete-led selection.

Organisers confirmed headline performers for the Milan opening show, naming Mariah Carey and Andrea Bocelli among the acts scheduled to appear; press materials and local reports suggested there may also be surprise additions to the line-up.

The torch relay has been extensive: organisers say there will be more than 10,000 torchbearers accompanying the Olympic flame as it travels around Italy, a relay that organisers have pitched as a way to place the Games in many communities across the country.

Early competition and interruptions

Curling, the only sport scheduled to be played every day of the Games, got under way early but was briefly disrupted when a power cut at the Olympic Ice Stadium in Cortina halted play across four sheets minutes into sessions. Competitors and officials paused as crews worked to restore power and organisers said play resumed shortly thereafter, but the stoppage highlighted the small, real-time vulnerabilities that can ripple across a packed schedule spanning multiple host towns.

The decision was taken following consultations with medical professionals after cases of norovirus were identified within Team Finland.

The IOC's statement accompanied confirmation that organisers had postponed a women's ice hockey preliminary match between Finland and Canada after cases of norovirus were identified in Team Finland. Canada are the reigning Olympic champions from 2022, and the delay therefore removed one of the earliest high-profile tests on the tournament calendar.

Officials pointed to what happened at Pyeongchang in 2018 when illness among staff required extensive measures: 41 security guards were hospitalised with vomiting and the IOC isolated about 1,200 others to prevent further spread. Medical advisers said a norovirus outbreak at Milano Cortina could likewise require isolation measures or lead to additional postponements if cases grow, and they emphasised that containment would be the immediate priority even as organisers evaluate operational impacts.

Athlete comebacks and injuries

My knee is not swollen, and with the help of a knee brace, I am confident that I can compete on Sunday,
— Lindsey Vonn

Lindsey Vonn crashed in a World Cup downhill in Crans-Montana, ending up in the safety nets. Teammates such as Bella Wright and Italian team physician Andrea Panzeri said Vonn has the mental state and clinical support to attempt a return, and some commentators suggested that Vonn's comeback, if it happens, could become one of the defining storylines at Milano Cortina. Independent medical analysts stressed that racing on a ruptured ACL would be unusual and that any start would hinge on specialist clearance and ongoing assessments.

Ice hockey returns to full strength

The National Hockey League has agreed to allow its players to participate in the Olympics for the first time since 2014, a move that organisers and writers said restores top professionals to the international tournament. Analysts argued this should make Olympic ice hockey a fuller test of the world's best, intensify rivalries and lift ticket demand, especially for marquee Canada and United States matchups. Some forecasters also predicted the NHL return could boost television ratings and wider global interest in the Olympic tournament, a development rights holders and broadcasters will watch closely.

Security, policing and protests

ICE does not and will never be able to carry out operational police activities on our national territory.
— Matteo Piantedosi, Italian interior minister

Matteo Piantedosi moved to blunt criticism, insisting ICE agents would not carry out operational policing on Italian soil and describing the concerns as misplaced. IOC president Kirsty Coventry called the focus on the ICE deployment "distracting and sad" and said the Games' magic would be what people remember once competition begins. Nevertheless, hundreds of demonstrators and a number of local political figures staged protests and publicly criticised the planned presence of ICE agents in Milan, and analysts warned that the controversy had already prompted political backlash that might influence how security is perceived. Organisers said they had drawn up a comprehensive security plan and had dispatched 6,000 police officers as part of opening-ceremony arrangements.

Broadcast, coverage and viewer tools

NBC's coverage in the United States will carry the opening ceremony live beginning at 2 p.m. ET on Feb. 6, with a prime-time presentation scheduled for 8 p.m. ET; the network and its Peacock streaming service are expected to use features such as Gold Zone and Multiview to let viewers follow multiple live feeds, curated medal windows and simultaneous finals. Broadcasters said they would organise staff and technical resources across dozens of venues to provide multi-camera feeds and highlight packages and to manage heavy, concurrent viewer demand.

Italy's organisers have set an internal target of 19 medals for the home side, a mark they acknowledge will be hard to reach given recent injuries and the depth of international fields. The programme also introduced several new medal competitions, including ski mountaineering, dual moguls, men's and women's doubles in luge, women's large-hill ski jumping and a team alpine combined event, widening medal opportunities and changing the strategic calculus for some teams.

State of play

Across reporting and official statements, it is confirmed that the Milano Cortina 2026 Games opened in Milan on Feb. 6, that curling and ice hockey had already started before the ceremony, and that organisers plan to run events through Feb. 22 with linked cauldrons in Milan and Cortina. Officials and broadcasters have supplied concrete details, from NBC's broadcast windows to national delegation sizes, and the IOC and medical teams have said cases of norovirus identified in Team Finland led to consultation and to the postponement of at least one match. Public-health advisers caution that further containment measures or schedule changes remain possible. Security officials say 6,000 police are deployed and Italy's interior minister maintains that foreign agents will not carry out operational policing on Italian soil, but opponents and local leaders continue to criticise the ICE presence; the coming days will determine whether outbreaks or demonstrations materially disrupt competition or timing.

Yonhap emphasised scale and host‑city allocations: organisers told reporters the Games opened with about 2,900 athletes from 92 countries spread over roughly 22,000 square kilometres, and that Milan will host all skating events and both the men's and women's hockey tournaments.

Western Europe
Sources
Die Welt (Germany), RTL Nieuws, SVT Sweden (State), Neue Zürcher Zeitung (NZZ)

Regional outlets emphasised operational details, inconsistent counts and early health or performance stories across neighbouring delegations.

  • Die Welt published two different figures for the German delegation (188 in one story, 183 in another) and explained that Leon Draisaitl and Katharina Schmid will parade at separate ceremony sites because opening events run simultaneously in multiple towns.
  • Die Welt also gave background on Leon Draisaitl's NHL contract, reporting an eight‑year deal with the Edmonton Oilers and an annual salary of about $14 million.
  • RTL reported short‑tracker Jens van 't Wout carried the Dutch flag into San Siro while skeleton athlete Kimberley Bos led the delegation in Cortina; 26 of the Netherlands' 39 athletes took part in opening ceremonies.
  • SVT described a heavy 0‑9 mixed‑doubles curling defeat for Sweden and said Isabella Wranå declined a post‑match interview, referring reporters to her brother.
  • NZZ reported the Swiss women's ice‑hockey team had a positive norovirus case, was isolated and did not attend the opening ceremony, yet went on to beat the Czech Republic 4‑3 after a penalty shootout.

Taken together these stories foreground logistics (simultaneous ceremonies), conflicting delegation figures and immediate health‑and‑performance angles that are shaping the first days of competition.

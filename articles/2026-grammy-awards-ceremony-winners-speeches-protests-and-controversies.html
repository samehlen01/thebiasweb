The 2026 Grammy Awards opened with major wins and politically charged acceptance speeches. Bad Bunny won Album of the Year for Debí Tirar Más Fotos. Billie Eilish won Song of the Year for 'Wildflower'. Kendrick Lamar won Record of the Year for 'luther' and increased his career Grammy tally. Olivia Dean was named Best New Artist. Lady Gaga won Best Pop Vocal Album.

Winners and milestones

Bad Bunny's Debí Tirar Más Fotos won Album of the Year, making him the first artist to win the prize with a record sung entirely in Spanish. He used his acceptance speech to criticize ICE and dedicate the award to immigrants.

Before I say thanks to God, I'm gonna say: ICE out!
— Bad Bunny

He also dedicated the award to people who had to leave their homelands to follow their dreams.

Political speeches and protests

Sara Fischer of Axios argued that awards shows have returned to political advocacy during the second Trump administration after a period of relative quiet.

Billie Eilish won Song of the Year for 'Wildflower' and used her acceptance moment to criticize ICE.

As grateful as I feel, I honestly don't feel like I need to say anything but that no one is illegal on stolen land.
— Billie Eilish

Portions of Eilish's anti-ICE expletive were censored on the live broadcast.

Multiple artists and attendees wore 'ICE OUT' pins on the red carpet and used acceptance moments and the telecast to criticize U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement.

DHS assistant secretary Tricia McLaughlin criticized Hollywood for 'demonizing' law enforcement in response to the Grammys' anti-ICE moments.

Host remarks and backlash

Trevor Noah opened the telecast with a monologue that targeted figures including Donald Trump and Nicki Minaj and included an Epstein-linked joke. Donald Trump denounced the joke and denied ever visiting Epstein Island. He said he would be sending his lawyers to sue Noah.

Those remarks presented conflicting accounts. Noah's monologue implied that Trump and others spent time on Epstein's island. Trump denied ever visiting Epstein Island and threatened legal action.

Red carpet reactions

Chappell Roan arrived on the red carpet in a sheer burgundy Mugler gown that exposed her nipple piercings; she later told E! the carpet felt 'overstimulating'. Body-language expert Judi James said Roan's posture made her look uncomfortable, vulnerable and out of sorts and suggested the look was intended to 'out-dare' other artists. One social-media critic wrote that the Grammys needed a dress code, and some observers suggested such a rule might follow.

Sabrina Carpenter finished her performance by climbing onto a plane prop and holding a white dove. PETA called the stunt cruel, saying bright lights, loud noise and handling cause fear and distress in birds and urging performers not to bring animals to the Grammys stage.

Stage moments and broadcasts

After fumbling with the envelope and joking about the teleprompter, the 79-year-old Cher accidentally announced 'Luther Vandross' as the winner before correcting to Kendrick Lamar.

Steven Spielberg won the Grammy for Best Music Film for Music By John Williams, achieving EGOT status. The song 'Golden' from K-Pop Demon Hunters, performed by the fictional trio Huntr/x, won Best Song Written for Visual Media and became the first K-Pop song to win a Grammy. Reba McEntire performed 'Trailblazer' during the In Memoriam segment and paid tribute to her late stepson Brandon Blackstock.

Lola Young won Best Pop Solo Performance for 'Messy'. She swore during her acceptance, with at least one expletive uncensored on some platforms.

An In Memoriam tribute to Ozzy Osbourne featured Post Malone, Slash and others and left Osbourne's family, including Sharon Osbourne, emotional.

Yungblud won Best Rock Performance for his live rendition of 'Changes'.

Justin Bieber performed 'Yukon' wearing only a pair of shorts and socks.

The ceremony lasted roughly three and a half hours and finished slightly behind schedule.

Following the three and a half hour telecast, the awards ceremony finished just eight minutes past the hour.

Tally disputes

Several outlets reported Kendrick Lamar increased his career Grammy total to 27, eclipsing Jay-Z's long-standing record of 25. Other outlets, however, reported a final tally of 26 Grammys.

Some coverage linked the anti-ICE statements at the Grammys to growing political pressure on immigration enforcement after recent Minneapolis shootings and ICE operations intensified calls for reform.

Speculation and immediate fallout

Donald Trump posted that Noah had made an incorrect statement about him and wrote that he would be sending his lawyers to sue the host. Observers speculated that Trump's lawyers would file suit, a possibility that remains unconfirmed.

An early-morning collision closed the M62 eastbound between Junction 34 (Selby) and Junction 35 (M18). Humberside Police urged motorists to avoid the area near J35 while officers dealt with the incident. The collision involved two cars and occurred at approximately 04:15am. National Highways said the carriageway had reopened and congestion was easing by around 9am. Drivers faced delays and were urged to avoid the area.

Timeline and response

National Highways said the M62 had overnight westbound closures between J27 and J26 for "technology works" and that it will be closed between J25 and J23 from 9pm until 6am for carriageway reconstruction.

Please be aware that the eastbound carriageway of M62 near to J35 is closed whilst police deal with an ongoing incident.
— Humberside Police

Traffic officers, contractors and recovery agents worked to clear the carriageway and inspect barrier damage.

Traffic impact and delays

National Highways estimated delays of 40 minutes eastbound between J24 and J26. It also reported delays of up to 20 minutes on the approach. On the westbound carriageway, two lanes were closed between J25 (Brighouse) and J24 (Ainley Top) because of a broken-down vehicle, causing slow traffic. Normal traffic conditions were expected to resume between 10.00 and 10.15.

Coverage and speculation

Coverage varied between outlets. The Sun described the collision as a "horror" traffic incident. The Sun reported that the incident was understood to have happened on an eastbound entry slip road and that it was expected to clear in the coming hours.

Diversion routes were put in place and drivers were warned to expect delays. Drivers faced delays and were urged to avoid the area.

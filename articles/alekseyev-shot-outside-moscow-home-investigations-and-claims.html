Who is Alekseyev?

Lt Gen Vladimir Alekseyev is widely reported to be the deputy head, effectively the number two, of Russia's GRU military intelligence. He has been described as a senior intelligence officer involved in operations during the 2022 invasion and as someone who negotiated with Yevgeny Prigozhin in 2023. Multiple outlets have reported that Alekseyev has been sanctioned by Western governments over alleged election interference and links to the Skripal Novichok case, with the New York Times referencing earlier sanctions tied to alleged meddling in the 2016 U.S. election while the Guardian cited alleged involvement connected to the 2020 vote.

Timing and possible motives

The shooting's timing drew attention because reports said it occurred the morning after senior GRU officials, including Igor Kostyukov, led a Russian delegation to talks with Ukrainian officials in Abu Dhabi. Russian officials have argued the timing shows the attack was aimed at derailing the negotiation process.

Official reactions

Senior Russian officials publicly blamed Ukraine for the attack. Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov accused Kyiv and said the shooting amounted to a terrorist act meant to disrupt negotiations.

Kremlin spokesman Dmitri Peskov said top Russian officers were definitely under threat and that decisions about relocating personnel were for the security services to make.

Alternative readings and criticism

Some analysts suggested the attack could stem from rivalries inside Russia's elite rather than from an external operation. They added, speculatively, that if Ukrainians were responsible it would demonstrate an ability to operate inside Moscow and point to a catastrophic security breach.

Pro-war commentators and military bloggers criticised authorities for apparent security lapses, calling the incident an example of criminal negligence.

Denys Prokopenko posted on X that if Alekseyev survived he would 'never sleep peacefully again'. That post was framed by some outlets as a sign that those he called war criminals would not feel safe and fed speculation that Alekseyev could be targeted again if he lives.

Speculation and unconfirmed leads

Kommersant, citing the Associated Press, reported that the attacker posed as a delivery person, shot Alekseyev twice in a stairwell and then shot him again as the two struggled. That account remains unconfirmed and is under investigation.

Security implications and consequences

Analysts and media commentators said the shooting would be seen as further evidence of failures by Russia's security services to protect senior military personnel and could intensify internal criticism. Security analysts warned the incident could trigger calls to relocate or better protect top officers or to change protection procedures.

Reporting also placed this event alongside a string of attacks and killings of high-ranking military officials in and around Moscow since February 2022.

State of play

What is confirmed is that Lt Gen Vladimir Alekseyev was shot several times in Moscow and taken to hospital. Investigators have opened a criminal case and are reviewing CCTV footage and interviewing witnesses. Russian officials, including Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov, publicly blamed Ukraine; Lavrov called the incident a terrorist act aimed at disrupting negotiations. Kremlin spokesman Dmitri Peskov said top officers were definitely under threat and that security services should make decisions on relocation. Analysts and some domestic commentators have offered alternative readings, including an internal power struggle. War supporters and military bloggers criticised the authorities for apparent security lapses and called this 'criminal negligence'. Some reports said Alekseyev had undergone surgery and remained in a coma. The Kommersant account that the attacker posed as a delivery worker has also been reported. That report remains unconfirmed and is under investigation. Investigators say operational searches and evidence review are under way and may produce more clarity.

Russia
Sources
Kommersant, Ria Novosti (State)
The investigation does not rule out that the attacker could have been connected to Ukraine's special services. If that version is confirmed, the criminal case could be reclassified as a terrorist act and state treason (Articles 205 and 275 of the Criminal Code of the Russian Federation).
— Moscow prosecutor's office

Russian outlets said the Moscow prosecutor's office has taken the probe under its control and opened a criminal case on attempted murder and illegal possession of firearms; officials flagged that the case could be upgraded to terrorism and state‑treason if evidence points to involvement by Ukrainian special services.

Kommersant cited an informed source suggesting the attacker may have been female, and a Russian public‑chamber official, Vladimir Rogov, said Alekseyev was in intensive care with three bullets in his chest.

Much of this framing appears in state and domestic media, a detail that would increase the political stakes if investigators confirm any link to Ukrainian services.

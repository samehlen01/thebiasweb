Coronation Street star Beverley Callard has revealed a breast cancer diagnosis. She said she learned the news about 15 to 20 minutes before filming her first scene for RTÉ's Fair City.

It’s very early stages, and I’m along with, thousands of other women as well.
— Beverley Callard, actress

Callard said the diagnosis came as she was preparing to go on set. She told reporters that tests had been done just before she left the UK and that she was in her dressing room at Fair City when she was notified.

But then I need an operation and some radiotherapy, and then I’m coming back to Fair City, so I will be back in just a few weeks.
— Beverley Callard, actress

She said she will travel back to the UK for further tests on lymph nodes and glands. She said she needs an operation and radiotherapy. She said she plans to return to Fair City in a few weeks.

RTÉ and Fair City confirmed Callard will make her debut as Lily on 19 February. She said she and her husband, Jon McEwan, will relocate to County Wicklow and have a house nearby.

Career and timeline

Some reports say Callard played Liz McDonald on Coronation Street from 1989 to 2020. Other reports describe her Coronation Street tenure as lasting 21 years.

Scheduling and broadcaster response

On Thursday, February 5, ITV pulled Coronation Street and Emmerdale from its schedule. They were replaced by the France v Ireland Guinness Six Nations match at the Stade de France in Paris. The France v Ireland match, which marks the beginning of the 2026 Six Nations tournament, will air on ITV1 and ITVX from 7.20pm until 10.35pm. Kevin Lygo said the new commissioning pattern is viewer-led and that ITVX gives viewers more choice. He said the company believes the number of episodes scheduled is the right amount fans can fit into their viewing schedules.

Public reaction and speculation

RTÉ's Late Late Show and many viewers sent Callard messages of support after her announcement. Fitness influencer Leigh Linton praised Callard and called her 'one of the strongest women I know'. Fans thanked Callard for sharing the news on her own terms, and some speculated her openness might encourage other women to get tested and catch this disease early.

The reaction underlines how public health disclosures intersect with popular culture: an actor arriving to work as a new character, and an entertainment schedule already shifting for live sport, mean personal health and programme planning have landed in the same headlines. That overlap has focused attention both on Callard’s immediate treatment plans and on how broadcasters balance live events with long-running serial drama.

What happens next

Callard said she will return to the UK for tests on lymph nodes and glands and then undergo an operation and radiotherapy before resuming work. RTÉ confirmed her debut will air on 19 February.

For viewers and colleagues the immediate focus is on Callard’s treatment and recovery, while broadcasters have set a clear airtime for her new role. ITV has defended a scheduling choice that moved two soaps to make way for sport, and that separate decision has its own supporters and critics inside the industry and among audiences.

State of play

It is confirmed that Beverley Callard has announced a breast cancer diagnosis. She has said the cancer is in the early stages and that the diagnosis came roughly 15 to 20 minutes before her first Fair City scene. She said she will undergo tests, an operation and radiotherapy and expects to return in a few weeks, and RTÉ has scheduled her debut as Lily for 19 February. Reporting differs on how long she played Liz McDonald, with some accounts giving the years 1989 to 2020 while others say 21 years, and broadcasters, programmes such as The Late Late Show, fitness influencer Leigh Linton and fans have offered support while some fans speculated her openness might prompt screening.

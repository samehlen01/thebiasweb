Nancy Guthrie, 84, was reported missing from her Tucson-area home after being last seen around 9:30 to 9:45pm on Saturday, and a family member called 911 around noon on Sunday, Pima County authorities said.

What investigators say

Authorities mounted an intensive search-and-rescue operation through the night, using drones, aircraft and helicopters along with dogs and heat and infrared sensors, the Pima County Sheriff's Office said. After searching all night, teams were sent home to rest. By Monday morning, Sheriff Chris Nanos told reporters that detectives had transitioned the inquiry from a missing-person search to a criminal investigation and that the Guthrie property was being treated as a crime scene. Nanos said investigators had found concerning evidence at the house but declined to provide details while homicide detectives assisted.

Conflicting wording from officials

Different outlets quoted Sheriff Chris Nanos' remarks differently. BBC and The Independent reported him as saying he believed Nancy Guthrie had been abducted and did not leave the house willingly. The Daily Mail reported that, when asked whether the term 'abduction' was appropriate, Nanos said it was not the right term and emphasized that investigators believed she could not have walked away.

Family and public reaction

Right now, our focus remains on the safe return of our dear mom.
— Savannah Guthrie

Savannah Guthrie released the family statement asking for prayers and urging anyone with information to contact the Pima County Sheriff's Department at (520) 351-4900. An NBC source told reporters she is expected to withdraw from her planned co-hosting duties at the Winter Olympics in Milan.

Health risk and profile

Investigators said Nancy Guthrie left her phone, wallet and car at the property and had not taken medication she needs. Sheriff Chris Nanos warned that the medication could be fatal if not taken within about 24 hours and urged urgency in the search. Deputies described Guthrie as a vulnerable adult because of mobility limitations but said she has no cognitive impairment and is of sound mind.

Search area and community reaction

Neighbors described the response as intense and out of character for the normally quiet Catalina Foothills neighborhood, and residents said they were very concerned for her. Officers drove up and down the street with lights flashing and a helicopter circled overhead while deputies went door-to-door asking to review footage from doorbell cameras and searched yards; they also checked local hospitals and medical transport teams.

It's a very safe neighborhood. This is highly unusual, and we are very concerned about her.
— Jeff Lamie, neighbor

Disputed scene details

Reports differ on visible signs inside the home. One account said lights in the common area were on, the front door was unlocked and there were signs of forced entry. Another said there were no obvious signs of forced entry, though elements of the scene caused grave concern and prompted homicide detectives to assist.

Federal help and leads

Multiple sources reported that the FBI and U.S. Customs and Border Protection and Border Patrol were assisting local authorities. Some reporting suggested investigators had narrowed a likely window to roughly 1 a.m. to 4 a.m., and that Guthrie may have been taken from her bed while she slept. Sheriff Nanos said the removal could involve more than one person. He said investigators would try to determine whether she had been stalked or harassed. He added that they were not aware of specific threats tied to her daughter's public profile.

Expert view and public support

Nicole Parker, a former FBI agent, told The Independent she believed the most likely suspect either knew Nancy Guthrie or the layout of the house and was familiar with its entry and exit points. Colleagues and other public figures shared the family's message on social media, asked followers to help and expressed prayers for Savannah and her relatives.

Investigative steps and public appeal

Authorities said they were processing the scene and following leads while search efforts were scaled back and teams were sent home to rest. They again asked anyone with information to contact the Pima County Sheriff's Department at (520) 351-4900.

Where things stand

Nancy Guthrie is missing. A broad night-time search took place. Investigators have treated the property as a crime scene. Federal agencies offered assistance. Officials and the family say she left personal items and medication behind. Savannah Guthrie has publicly appealed for help. The sheriff has expressed the view that she may have been abducted. His precise wording was reported differently in separate accounts. Reports differ on whether there were obvious signs of forced entry. Other accounts say there were no obvious signs despite elements of the scene that caused grave concern. Investigators are reviewing surveillance and following leads. This continues even as search operations have been scaled back.

Santander said 96% of its transactions are now completed in digital channels.

In response to a continuing and sizeable shift towards customers using digital banking, we are making changes to our branches to better support our customers.
— Santander spokeswoman

Santander said closing branches will be replaced with "community bankers" operating from Santander Locals or banking hubs. It added that wherever a store is closing, a Santander representative will operate from a nearby banking hub or Santander Local - locations in libraries and community centres where customers can seek help one day each week.

Santander said customers can continue to bank at more than 11,000 Post Office branches nationwide and that all closing branches are reportedly within 1.4 miles of a nearby Post Office.

Santander said the refreshed network will consist of around 244 full branches plus counter-free, reduced-hour formats, work cafés and Santander Locals. Santander said it is continuing to invest tens of millions of pounds into its branches, refurbishing more than 220 sites over the past six years. Santander has set up a dedicated phone line for customers seeking more information about the closures.

Reactions from communities and officials

Local customers and politicians voiced strong criticism. Mike Thompson said "I am absolutely appalled. Me and my family are absolutely devastated by the news and I will be moving my money somewhere else." Deborah Erskine (MLA) said "Bank closures like this have serious consequences for people and businesses and pushing customers towards online banking is not an option for everyone." Ministers have criticised the closure of bank branches, arguing it restricts access to cash for elderly and vulnerable people.

Local impact and mitigation

With several banks choosing to close branches across the UK, concerns have been raised about "banking deserts", with parts of Yorkshire leaving people without a bank for 10 miles. A solution in the form of 'banking hubs' exists but rollout has been slow; there are currently around 210 hubs open across the UK. LINK has announced plans to deliver a new banking hub in Whitehaven following the Santander branch closure there.

Speculation and wider context

The latest closures could take the number of jobs axed by the bank in the UK over the 3,000 mark in the past few years. Last July, UK boss Mike Regnier said they had cut around 2,000 roles over the previous year, but indicated there "might well be" more job cuts on the way. The closures come as Santander moves closer to completing its takeover of TSB for £2.65 billion. The deal with TSB, which will bring more branches into the business, is expected to complete later this quarter.

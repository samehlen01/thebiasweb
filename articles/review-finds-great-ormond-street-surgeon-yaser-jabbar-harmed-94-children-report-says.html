Review finds Great Ormond Street surgeon Yaser Jabbar harmed 94 children, the hospital announced after publishing an independent review into his cases. The review covered 789 children who were seen by Jabbar between 2017 and 2022. It concluded that 91 of the harmed patients had been operated on by Jabbar.

What the review found

The independent panel classified levels of harm as 36 severe, 39 moderate and 19 mild. The report identified widespread unacceptable practice, including poor planning before surgery, wrong-level bone cuts, implants placed in the wrong position and problems with pins and complication management. It found children suffered chronic pain, deformities, permanent nerve damage, amputation and leg-length differences of up to 20cm.

GOSH said it could not determine whether all the cases of harm were avoidable.

Numbers and reporting differences

GOSH said it ordered a review of 721 patients and later identified 68 further cases, bringing the total reviewed to 789. Other reporting described the review as covering 723 children.

The hospital's headline figure is 94 children harmed. One report said the number harmed was between 85 and 100. Some accounts said 36 patients suffered severe harm. Another account said 35 patients had suffered severe harm.

Hospital response and apology

We are profoundly sorry to all the patients and their families who have been affected by the care provided by Mr Jabbar, an Orthopaedic Surgeon at our hospital between 2017 - 2022.
— Matthew Shaw, chief executive, Great Ormond Street Hospital

Mr Shaw apologised on behalf of the trust and said the report set out what happened, what was found, what had been learnt and what the trust had done in response. He said the hospital had acted quickly once concerns were raised.

Changes already made

GOSH said it had acted on Royal College of Surgeons recommendations by introducing training on handling complaints, investing more in whistle-blower support and requiring surgeons to discuss the most complex cases with the National Royal Orthopaedic Hospital.

Families, critics and legal action

Some families said an apology is not enough. Dean Stalham said the findings were too little and too late. Lizzie Roberts said the hospital had effectively marked its own homework.

An independent review concluded that Vivaan Sharma suffered moderate harm and that the surgery carried out was incorrect and unsuitable. Vivaan's father, Viresh Sharma, rejected that classification. He said his boy will be affected for life. Caroline Murgatroyd said solicitors will pursue damages on behalf of affected families. She added that the report may offer reassurance but provides little comfort to many parents. Ms Murgatroyd also warned that Great Ormond Street Hospital's reputation will be tarnished for a long time. Some families speculated that a cover-up culture could obscure the truth.

Regulatory and criminal steps

A prior Royal College of Surgeons review described some surgeries as inappropriate and incorrect and raised serious concerns about working culture in the department. Before concerns were raised in June 2022 the trust said it had received seven complaints about Jabbar and investigated one serious incident that was signed off by NHS England. The Metropolitan Police said it will review the GOSH report and assess whether police involvement is required. Jabbar is understood to be living abroad and no longer has a licence to practise medicine in the UK.

Legal outlook and next steps

Solicitors have said they will pursue damages on behalf of clients affected by the care under review. The Metropolitan Police review may lead to further action depending on its assessment of the report. GOSH says it has introduced changes intended to reduce the chance of recurrence.

State of play

The independent review concluded that 94 children had been harmed. The hospital has apologised. Families and campaigners say the response is inadequate, solicitors have said they will pursue damages, and the Metropolitan Police will review the report to determine whether police involvement is required.

Gwent Police opened a murder investigation after a 17-year-old was found dead at a property on Lower Francis Street in Abertridwr at about 5.45pm on 5 February. The teenager has been named as Tristan Shae Kerr.

Five people have been arrested and a murder investigation has been launched after a 17-year-old died.
— BBC - UK

The arrests included three men: a 26-year-old from London, a 24-year-old from Abertridwr and an 18-year-old from Cardiff, who were held on suspicion of murder. Two women, a 28-year-old from Hereford and a 24-year-old from Senghenydd, were arrested on suspicion of assisting an offender. Police said all five suspects remained in custody as enquiries continued.

The incident took place at a property on Lower Francis Street at around 5.45pm on Thursday 5 February.
— South Wales Argus

Gwent Police said officers were called to the address at about 17:45 on Thursday after reports the teenager had sustained a serious injury.

He was pronounced dead at the scene.
— Gwent Police

Police said the boy's family had been informed and were being supported by specialist officers.

Tristan Shae Kerr, 17, was fatally wounded in a terraced property in his hometown of Abertridwr, South Wales on Thursday.
— Metro

Some local reports said he had been fatally wounded. The precise cause of Tristan's death has not been released and remains unconfirmed publicly.

Police were knocking on doors as investigations were carried out, and it was apparent they were trying to obtain Ring doorbell footage.
— South Wales Argus

A white tent had also been erected outside the property where the boy was found dead.

Community reaction

Local residents and community groups said they were in shock and paid tributes. Family and friends described Tristan as a 'cheeky chappie' and a 'precious boy' who had his whole life ahead of him. His former Senghenydd rugby club and a former coach remembered him as a vibrant, talented player. Friends and relatives said they were utterly shattered by his death, and some friends urged each other to put down knives in the wake of the killing.

Gwent Police warned that speculation about the identities of those involved could present problems and hinder the investigation, and said residents were likely to see an increased police presence while enquiries continue.

The teenager’s friend Jack set up a GoFundMe in his memory looking to raise £7,500 ($10,195) to help his mother with funeral costs.
— Metro

The page seeks to raise £7,500 ($10,195) to help Tristan's mother with funeral costs, the organiser said.

Anyone with information is asked to contact Gwent Police by calling 101, sending a direct message on social media, or submitting information via the force’s website, quoting log reference 2600038325.
— South Wales Argus

The log reference given by the force is 2600038325 and Crimestoppers can be contacted on 0800 555 111, police said.

State of play

Authorities have confirmed a murder investigation is under way. The 17-year-old victim has been identified as Tristan Shae Kerr. Five people remain in police custody as enquiries continue. Gwent Police warned that public speculation about who was involved could harm the probe. They said officers were likely to keep a visible presence in the area. Family, friends and local clubs have expressed shock and grief. A friend has launched a GoFundMe to raise £7,500 ($10,195) to help with funeral costs. The precise cause of Tristan's death has not been released and remains unconfirmed. Anyone with information is asked to contact Gwent Police on 101 or via the force website, quoting log reference 2600038325, or call Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

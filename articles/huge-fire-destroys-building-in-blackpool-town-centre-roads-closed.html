Huge fire destroys building in Blackpool town centre and forces road closures. A commercial property on Waterloo Road in Blackpool caught fire late on February 6 and into the morning of February 7. Lancashire Fire and Rescue Service mobilised 15 fire pumps to the scene.

Fire service response

Lancashire Fire and Rescue Service said 15 fire pumps were mobilised to the scene. Crews used breathing apparatus and ground monitors while tackling the blaze. The service said the incident was complex. It cited the size of the premises, the severity of the fire and concerns about structural stability.

15
Fire pumps in attendance
Reported at peak attendance

That figure was given in public updates by the service and reflected the peak deployment while crews were tackling the blaze.

The scene is complex given the size of the premises, severity of the fire and structural stability. We will continue to monitor and firefight throughout the night and into the morning.
— Lancashire Fire and Rescue Service

Road closures were put in place on Gordon Street and on stretches of Waterloo Road and Rawcliffe Street as crews worked and the public were asked to avoid the area. Road closures and smoke disrupted travel in the immediate area. Authorities urged people to keep windows and doors closed if they could see or smell smoke. Firefighters remained on site overnight while appliances were gradually reduced and, by the following evening, two engines and an aerial ladder platform remained.

Local impact and evacuations

Numerous nearby properties were evacuated and residents were relocated from their homes while crews fought the blaze. Local reports said residents were moved to temporary accommodation or away from the immediate area. Business owners and officials emphasised that, importantly, no one had been reported injured.

Businesses, damage and fundraising

The blaze affected several businesses along Waterloo Road. These include Smart Mart, Smart Play Blackpool and the South Shore Pitstop Café. Smart Play Blackpool said the building and play area have been completely destroyed. Its team said they were absolutely devastated. Owners warned that parties and bookings would need to be cancelled or relocated and that deposits would be refunded.

We are absolutely devastated to share that there has been a fire at the play area.
— Smart Play Blackpool

A fundraiser page has been launched to support Smart Mart and Smart Play as they recover. Organisers said donations would be used for recovery costs and that any surplus would be split between Smart Mart and South Shore Pitstop Community Café. The owner of Smart Mart said he would refund deposits, pledged to fundraise for the community and said he would try to help affected local businesses get back up and running. He also said he hoped to open temporary bed centre and sofa centre spaces while the business recovers.

It was Smart Mart that was on fire and the gaff's in bits.
— Steve (Smart Mart owner)

In a social media video the owner said the premises had been badly damaged and stressed that no one had been hurt; he said he planned to return deposits and to support local recovery through fundraising. Organisers and owners emphasised the refunds and community support plans are intended to help customers and neighbours in the short term while longer rebuilding work is planned.

What happens next

Lancashire Fire and Rescue Service said it will continue to work with partner agencies to support residents and businesses and bring the incident to a safe conclusion as quickly as possible. Road closures remain in place on Gordon Street and sections of Waterloo Road and Rawcliffe Street while crews continue to monitor the site and reduce risks.

State of play

Across reports, a commercial property on Waterloo Road caught fire late on February 6 into the morning of February 7. Emergency crews attended in force, with 15 fire pumps mobilised at the peak of the response. No injuries have been reported. Officials said road closures and evacuations were necessary. They added they will work with partner agencies as crews continue to monitor the damaged site.

Actor and comedian Catherine O'Hara dies aged 71. Her agency, CAA, said on Friday she died at her Los Angeles home after a brief illness.

Prolific multi-award-winning actress, writer, and comedian Catherine O’Hara died today at her home in Los Angeles following a brief illness.
— CAA (agency statement)

The Los Angeles Fire Department said crews responded at 4:48 a.m. to a request for medical aid at an address in Brentwood and transported an approximately 70-year-old female in serious condition. Published dispatch audio included the call 'Engine rescue 19, breathing difficulty 575'.

Those accounts do not line up. The agency's release said she died at home after a brief illness, while the fire department's statement and the recorded dispatch describe a medical response and transport from a Brentwood residence. Separately, several media outlets have speculated that she experienced a medical emergency, had difficulty breathing and was taken to hospital before being pronounced dead.

No cause of death has been publicly revealed.

CAA said the family will hold a private celebration of life. She is survived by her husband, Bo Welch, and their sons, Matthew and Luke. Other outlets named siblings Michael O’Hara, Mary Margaret O'Hara and Maureen Jolley among surviving relatives. Additional reports listed Marcus O‘Hara, Tom O'Hara and Patricia Wallice as family members who survive her.

Career and honours

A Canadian-born performer, O'Hara first came to notice with Toronto's Second City troupe and later as a regular on the sketch series SCTV, launching a career that stretched across five decades.

Her work earned major industry recognition: she won Emmy awards and a Golden Globe, with some of the most recent honours tied to her performance on Schitt's Creek.

Many viewers remember her turns in films including Home Alone and Beetlejuice.

Production notes and final appearances

Industry reports said production on season two of a project called The Studio was adjusted in mid-January to avoid scenes with her character. An unnamed production source told tabloids that she may not have filmed any scenes for the new season, a detail reported but not independently confirmed.

Her last public appearance came at the Emmys in September, where some outlets said she appeared frail on the red carpet.

Tributes and reactions

Tributes and memories poured in on social media and from colleagues across film and television.

  • Macaulay Culkin said he had thought they had more time together and expressed deep personal grief and love.
  • Seth Rogen described her as hysterical, kind, intuitive and generous, and said her presence pushed others to do their best.
  • Meryl Streep praised O'Hara's whipsmart compassion and the warmth she brought to the eccentric people she portrayed.
  • Pedro Pascal called her a genius and said he was eternally grateful to have been near her work and presence.

Others recalled long collaborations and family-like ties.

  • Dan Levy said it had been a gift to work with O'Hara for decades and that she felt like family long before she ever played his on-screen relative.
  • Christopher Guest said, plainly, that 'we have lost one of the comic giants of our age,' reflecting the breadth of her career.
  • Ron Howard called her a wonderful collaborator and sent his condolences to Bo Welch and the family.
  • Michael Keaton, who knew her before Beetlejuice, said the news 'hurts' and that he will miss her.

State of play

Multiple outlets report that Catherine O'Hara has died and that her family will hold a private celebration of life; at the same time, the agency's statement that she died at home after a brief illness sits alongside Los Angeles Fire Department records and dispatch audio describing a medical response and transport at 4:48 a.m., and no official cause of death has been released. Media reports and unnamed production sources have speculated about a medical emergency and about her absence from early filming for a new season, but those accounts remain unconfirmed while awards listings still show her as a nominee and the family asks for privacy.

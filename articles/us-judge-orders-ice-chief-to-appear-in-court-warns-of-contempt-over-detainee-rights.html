US judge orders ICE chief to appear in court and warns of contempt over detainee rights. Chief Judge Patrick J. Schiltz directed acting Immigration and Customs Enforcement director Todd Lyons to appear in federal court on Friday for a contempt hearing after concluding ICE had failed to comply with court orders. The order followed a Jan. 14 grant of a petition that required a detained petitioner be given a bond hearing within seven days, and on Jan. 23 counsel notified the court that the petitioner remained in custody; Schiltz said he would cancel Lyons' appearance if that detainee is released from custody.

Judge's written findings

Chief Judge Patrick J. Schiltz wrote in court filings that ICE's failure to follow court directives has routinely inflicted hardship on noncitizens, including people who have long lived and worked in the United States.

The practical consequence of respondents' failure to comply has almost always been significant hardship to aliens (many of whom have lawfully lived and worked in the United States for years and done absolutely nothing wrong).
— Chief Judge Patrick J. Schiltz

Personnel moves and conflicting reports

President Donald Trump said he sent border adviser Tom Homan to Minnesota to take over the administration's immigration enforcement there and that he had sent Homan 'to de-escalate a little bit.'

Media outlets reported that Gregory Bovino and some federal agents were expected to leave Minneapolis and that The Atlantic reported Bovino had been demoted, but the Department of Homeland Security disputed that account and DHS spokesperson Tricia McLaughlin wrote on X that Bovino 'has NOT been relieved of his duties.'

Analysts disagreed on the significance of any personnel changes: Ruiz Soto speculated that sidelining Bovino could signal ruptures within the Trump administration and an effort to impose more accountability, while Lucas Guttentag said swapping Bovino for Homan alone would not, on its own, amount to a meaningful policy shift.

Minneapolis shooting and evidence access

The Department of Homeland Security said Pretti approached federal officers on the street with a 9-millimeter semiautomatic handgun and 'violently resisted' when officers tried to disarm him.

But videos and witness accounts recorded at the scene show Pretti was holding a phone, not a handgun, that agents shoved and pepper-sprayed him, forced him to the ground, and that an agent secured a handgun before a shot was fired less than a second later.

Federal officials denied Minnesota state investigators access to the shooting scene in south Minneapolis, prompting local and state officials to accuse the Homeland Security agency of mishandling evidence.

The fatal shootings of Renee Good and Alex Pretti this month have sparked protests in Minneapolis and around the nation.

Ecuador's foreign ministry said consular staff prevented an ICE officer from entering the Minneapolis consulate and immediately submitted a note of protest to the U.S. Embassy in Ecuador. Video shows dozens of immigrant families protesting at a detention facility in Texas after reports that a 5-year-old Ecuadorian boy was taken with his father following their detention in Minnesota.

Political reactions and divisions

DHS spokesperson Tricia McLaughlin accused the judge of being more concerned about politics than the safety of the Minnesotans. The White House's Karoline Leavitt defended Gregory Bovino as a capable professional who should continue to lead Customs and Border Patrol. Ben Cohen, co‑founder of Ben & Jerry's, called for Immigration and Customs Enforcement to be defunded and disbanded. House Democratic Leader Hakeem Jeffries said Kristi Noem should be fired and threatened that Democrats would commence impeachment proceedings.

New York Governor Kathy Hochul said the fastest way to help people during the blizzard was for ICE to 'back off' so they would feel safe accessing warming centers, shelters, hospitals, and houses of worship. A source familiar with the situation said Hochul never rejected federal assistance and that Albany supported all storm-related requests from local governments. The governor's remarks drew a rebuke from White House spokesman Kush Desai and criticism from the Staten Island Young Republicans and conservative strategist Steve Guest, who said the governor was prioritizing politics over her constituents; Hochul campaign spokesperson Ryan Radulovacki defended the governor and attacked critics including Bruce Blakeman.

What happens next

Courts have ordered Lyons to appear on Friday as the case surrounding a Jan. 14 petition and a Jan. 23 counsel's notice moves forward; Schiltz said he would cancel that appearance if the petitioner is released. Federal and independent accounts still clash — notably over whether Alex Pretti was armed, because federal officials say he had a 9-millimeter semiautomatic handgun while videos and witnesses record him holding a phone, and over state investigators' access to the shooting scene. The immediate next step is the contempt hearing scheduled for Friday, which could be mooted if the government provides the ordered bond hearing or releases the detainee.

A serious collision early on Wednesday, February 4, closed the M11 southbound between junction 7 (Harlow) and junction 6 (M25 Interchange), after a vehicle overturned and ended up on its roof on the verge, National Highways said. The collision was reported at 12.50am on Wednesday, February 4. Emergency services and National Highways Traffic Officers were on scene helping to manage traffic. National Highways urged rush-hour motorists to plan ahead and said delays of more than an hour were likely.

National Highways Traffic Officers were helping to manage traffic at the scene, and motorists were being routed along a diversion signed with the Solid Circle symbol on road signs.

Traffic and diversions

Officials said they were making plans to release traffic trapped within the closure.

Queues and airport disruption

Traffic cameras showed long queues, and MyLondon reported massive tailbacks that were expected to last throughout the day. There were also reports of ongoing delays for travellers heading to Stansted Airport.

What this means for commuters

National Highways urged rush-hour motorists to plan ahead and said delays of more than an hour were possible. The agency said its Traffic Officers were at the scene and drivers were being diverted along a signed route marked with the Solid Circle symbol.

Traffic cameras and local reports showed long queues; MyLondon said massive tailbacks had formed and were expected to last throughout the day. National Highways warned of delays of more than an hour, and officials said they were planning to release traffic that had been trapped within the closure.

Timeline and reporting differences

National Highways recorded the collision at 12.50am on Wednesday, February 4. The Sun - News published an uncertain report saying emergency services raced to the scene just before 1am and that lanes had reopened. By contrast, National Highways and MyLondon said the carriageway remained closed and the closure was still in effect several hours after the collision.

That difference, with one account saying lanes had reopened and another saying the carriageway remained closed, is a substantive discrepancy about the road's status. Readers should note the claim types: The Sun's report is listed as uncertain in the bundle, while National Highways issued official statements.

Coverage and reaction

Coverage varied: The Sun - News used an emphatic headline, while National Highways emphasised traffic-management measures and urged drivers to plan ahead.

People and status

It is not yet known whether the driver or any passengers were injured.

State of play

National Highways said the M11 southbound was closed between junction 7 (Harlow) and junction 6 (M25 Interchange) after a vehicle overturned, and that its traffic officers were managing a signed diversion while officials planned to release traffic trapped by the closure. An uncertain report from The Sun - News said emergency services arrived just before 1am and that lanes had reopened, which conflicts with National Highways' account. National Highways indicated the closure was expected to continue while police investigated. The status of the driver and any passengers is not yet known, and authorities warned that delays of more than an hour were likely.

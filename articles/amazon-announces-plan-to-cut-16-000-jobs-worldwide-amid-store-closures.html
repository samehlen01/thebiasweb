Amazon announces plan to cut 16,000 jobs worldwide, the company said, marking a fresh round of reductions that follows October's roughly 14,000 job cuts and brings planned losses over recent months to about 30,000, Sky News concluded.

What Amazon said

In a staff memo, Beth Galetti, Amazon's senior vice president for people experience and technology, said the moves aim to streamline management layers, increase individual ownership and cut bureaucracy; she added that Amazon will continue hiring and investing in strategic areas that are critical to its future and will offer most US-based employees 90 days to look for a new role inside the company.

The reductions we are making today will impact approximately 16,000 roles across Amazon, and we're again working hard to support everyone whose role is impacted,
— Beth Galetti, senior vice president, Amazon

Galetti said the new round is focused on teams that had not completed reorganizations linked to the October reductions.

Layoff logistics and store closures

Amazon also said it will close its Amazon Go and Amazon Fresh physical stores, convert some locations into Whole Foods Market stores while keeping Amazon Fresh as an online brand, and eliminate roles across its grocery organisation.

It plans to shut 57 Amazon Fresh stores and 15 Amazon Go locations in February, and it said it will open more than 100 new Whole Foods Market stores over the next few years.

Jason Buechel, who leads the grocery business, said the closures required eliminating roles and that the company would support impacted employees with 90 days of pay and benefits, career transition services and severance packages.

Amazon invited affected employees to reapply for other Amazon positions, though openings were limited, and employees who did not move to another role were offered severance based on their tenure.

Internal draft email and 'Project Dawn'

Reporters said a draft internal email was apparently shared by mistake and quickly cancelled; that email referred to employees in the US, Canada and Costa Rica as having been laid off.

The draft appears to have been written by Colleen Aubrey and was linked to a calendar invitation titled Send project Dawn email, an apparent reference to Amazon's internal code name for the cuts.

Wider impact and shipping partners

Outside Amazon, UPS said it will cut up to 30,000 jobs this year as it further reduces shipments for its biggest customer, Amazon; Pitney Bowes' parcel index also noted that Amazon handled 6.3 billion deliveries in the United States in 2024 and is projected to overtake the USPS for US delivery volumes by 2028.

Reactions and analysis

Voices across the policy and business communities differed on causes and consequences. Axios argued the cuts fit a broader pattern in which companies try to do more with fewer people — citing AI-driven productivity gains, post-pandemic over-hiring and inflation — and described the moment as part of a 'forever layoff' era. A former Amazon employee said the 16,000 reductions had been expected by staff for weeks. Some insiders and commentators speculated that further rounds could push total cuts toward roughly 30,000. Executives at the World Economic Forum said AI would both eliminate and create roles, though two told Reuters that AI might be used as an excuse by companies planning cuts. Jason Buechel said he was genuinely optimistic about the future of Amazon's grocery business despite the Fresh and Go closures.

State of play

Reporting across outlets agrees that Amazon has announced plans to eliminate 16,000 roles and that the company will offer most US-based staff a 90-day internal job-search window and provide support including pay, benefits and career-transition services to impacted employees. Outlets have also published a draft email that was apparently sent by mistake and referenced employees in the US, Canada and Costa Rica, and a calendar entry titled Send project Dawn email has been tied to the process; former employees and some analysts speculate further rounds could bring total cuts nearer to 30,000. Partners such as UPS have said they will cut up to 30,000 jobs this year as they reduce shipments for Amazon, and commentators remain divided over whether AI is the proximate cause or a convenient rationale for planned reductions.

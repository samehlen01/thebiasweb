Golden Globes moment

Owen Cooper, the 16-year-old actor from Warrington, took home a Golden Globe on Sunday for his supporting role in Netflix's limited series Adolescence. The award came at the 83rd Golden Globe Awards in Los Angeles and Cooper closed his acceptance speech with a nod to Liverpool Football Club’s anthem.

The speech

Bring on 2026. You'll never walk alone.
— Owen Cooper

During his acceptance remarks Cooper said he had 'taken a risk' by signing up for drama classes where he was 'the only boy' and that he still considered himself 'an apprentice' learning from more experienced performers.

Teacher and local reactions

Esther Morgan, co‑founder of Manchester's The Drama MOB and Cooper's former teacher, told Metro that he had been 'brilliant' and that his success had sparked a 'great uptake of boys' at their school. Metro also cited reviews calling his debut 'potentially the best debut ever seen by a child actor' and other critics 'phenomenal' and 'flawless'. Local coverage described him as 'really grounded' and quoted his teacher saying his parents had 'kept his feet on the ground'.

Awards haul and reporting differences

Adolescence's awards night haul included Stephen Graham winning Best Actor in a TV limited series, Erin Doherty taking Best Supporting Actress and the show being named Best Limited Series. Reporting differs on the number of trophies: Wakefield Express said the four‑part series 'took home a total of four gongs', while Metro reported it 'took home every award it was up for'. There is also a mismatch over character names: several outlets name Cooper's role as Jamie Miller, but Wakefield Express called the part 'Charlie'. Outlets also disagreed about the record he set. The Daily Star described his win as making him 'the youngest winner in the ceremony’s history'. The Warrington Guardian called him 'the second‑youngest male winner', and the Daily Mail used similar 'second‑youngest' phrasing.

Context and what's next

Production details varied in reporting: Wakefield Express said the four‑part series was filmed at Production Park in South Kirkby over six months in 2024. Metro reported Cooper has a film debut lined up in Emerald Fennell’s Wuthering Heights adaptation and that co‑creator Stephen Graham pushed to cast an unknown northern actor. Broad coverage also reminded readers that Cooper had already won an Emmy and Critics' Choice awards and that he was still studying for his GCSEs.

Tesla to end Model S and Model X production as it pivots toward Optimus robots. Elon Musk said the company will stop making the Model S and Model X and refit its Fremont production lines to build Optimus humanoid robots, describing the move on Tesla's quarterly earnings call. Musk said Tesla will replace the S and X production line 'with a 1 million unit per year line of Optimus.'

It's time to basically bring the Model S and X programs to an end with an honorable discharge. If you're interested in buying a Model S and X, now would be the time to order it.
— Elon Musk, CEO

Tesla said it will retool the Fremont lines with the aim of producing Optimus at scale, and the company plans to unveil a third-generation Optimus design this quarter described as its 'first design meant for mass production.'

Tesla's Model 3 and Model Y accounted for 97% of the company's 1.59 million deliveries last year, and in 2025 the firm delivered 1,585,279 Model 3 and Y vehicles versus 418,227 Model S and X units.

Why Tesla says it's shifting

Elon Musk framed the shift as strategic, saying Optimus could boost long-term US GDP and 'create amazing abundance,' and Tesla said it would invest about $2 billion in Musk's AI startup xAI to help the company 'develop and deploy AI products and services into the physical world at scale.'

Tesla also reported mixed profit figures: net income in the quarter plunged 61% to $840 million, and the company said its 2025 profit fell 46% to $3.8 billion; the two numbers refer to different reporting periods and should not be read as a direct contradiction.

Investments, spending and funding

Tesla said it entered an agreement to invest about $2 billion in xAI, and CFO Vaibhav Taneja told investors to expect roughly $20 billion in capital expenditures this year focused on new factories, Optimus and artificial intelligence computing resources; Taneja also said the company has more than $44 billion in cash and investments that could be used to fund those plans.

Andrew Rocco called the planned $20 billion in capex 'necessary spending' and said it increases his confidence that Musk's timelines will be honoured; shareholders last year also approved a record pay package for Musk that could be worth nearly $1tn, and Musk told investors some projects were being undertaken 'out of desperation,' a remark outlets treated as speculative.

Product roadmap and Robotaxi

Tesla said it has begun tooling ahead of Cybercab production and expects the two-seat, purpose-built driverless vehicle to begin production by April; the Cybercab will not include a steering wheel or pedals.

Tesla operates a Robotaxi-branded pilot in Austin and has run some driverless passenger rides without human safety supervisors as part of its autonomous services testing.

Musk warned that Optimus requires a new supply chain because 'there's really nothing from the existing supply chain that exists in Optimus,' and he also claimed the company expects to make 'far more Cybercabs than all of our other vehicles combined.' Multiple outlets and analysts have expressed skepticism that Optimus can be mass-produced and commercially successful.

Reactions and debate

Andrew Rocco hailed the spending as necessary and said it bolsters confidence in Musk's timelines. Engadget argued Optimus has failed to meet expectations in demonstrations and that Musk's timelines are often overly optimistic. CNBC suggested some of Tesla's 2025 problems were related to Musk's political activity and said that prompted a consumer backlash. Mashable warned the S and X discontinuation will likely upset fans.

Musk said a lot of investors asked Tesla to participate in xAI funding and that the company was following shareholders' wishes.

Tesla's shares rose about 2% in extended trading after the earnings report, and a recent shareholder vote on a proposal to invest in xAI saw abstentions and votes against outnumber approvals.

What is known and what comes next

Company statements confirm Tesla will end Model S and X production and retool Fremont to make Optimus. The company said it plans to unveil a third-generation Optimus this quarter and has announced an investment in xAI alongside large planned capital spending to support the push. Executives reported a full-year revenue decline, and the filings showed net income in the quarter plunged 61% to $840 million while 2025 profit fell 46% to $3.8 billion; Elon Musk and supporters said Optimus could boost long-term US GDP and 'create amazing abundance,' while critics argued Optimus has underperformed in demonstrations and may not be commercially viable. Key unknowns include how quickly Optimus or Cybercab production can scale, how a new supply chain will be built, and how the spending will be financed; investors will watch the Optimus unveiling, Cybercab tooling progress and Tesla's capex disclosures for signals.

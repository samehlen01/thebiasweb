The event Portsmouth v Southampton South Coast derby ends in draw as police make arrests after disorder unfolded on Sunday 25 January. The match finished 1-1, with Ebou Adams equalising in the 77th minute after Leo Scienza put Southampton ahead in the 57th. Police said five people were arrested following incidents connected to the fixture.

Police orders and powers

Hampshire and Isle of Wight Constabulary authorised a Section 34 dispersal order for a 12-hour period, running from 7am to 7pm on matchday and covering the area surrounding the ground. A Section 60AA order operated during the same hours and allowed officers to require people to remove face coverings or anything used to conceal their identity. Police warned that failure to comply with a dispersal order is a criminal offence and could result in arrest. Hampshire Police also pledged to stop those intent on causing disruption.

From our policing perspective this has been another successful operation with the match passing without any major disruption.
— Chief Superintendent Simon Dodds

Chief Superintendent Simon Dodds said the tactic could give officers another means to respond quickly and he hoped it might send a clear message to prevent disruption.

On the ground

A large visible policing operation was deployed around Fratton Park, including mounted police, motorbike outriders, police dogs, a helicopter and a motorcade escorting visiting coaches. The operation accompanied the arrival of around 2,700 Southampton supporters.

Several roads, including Rodney Road, were closed and some fenced areas had black mesh stretched to prevent rival fans seeing each other. Rodney Road and surrounding routes were shut to allow Southampton coaches to arrive and leave while keeping fans apart.

A 36-year-old man from Southampton was arrested on suspicion of common assault and has been bailed while enquiries continue. A 32-year-old man from Portsmouth was arrested on suspicion of going on to the playing area and has been bailed while enquiries continue. A 28-year-old man from Portsmouth was arrested on suspicion of going on to the playing area and has been bailed while enquiries continue. A 34-year-old man from Portsmouth was arrested on suspicion of going on to the playing area and remains in custody. A 19-year-old man from Portsmouth was arrested on suspicion of throwing an object and remains in custody.

Background and reaction

The clubs and the police said they were determined to avoid a repeat of the 2019 Fratton Park clashes. When those teams met at Fratton Park in 2019 there was fighting outside the stadium, a number of people were injured and eight men were later jailed for violent disorder.

Susan French said she expected a nervy game and predicted a 2-2 draw. Lucy French said she was confident and predicted a 3-1 home win. Libby French said she expected a 2-1 victory for Saints. Portsmouth News said fans were in fine spirits, packing into the Fans Zone and areas around the ground ahead of kick-off.

First Bus offered free bus travel for Pompey fans within the city on matchday.

The draw lifted Portsmouth out of the relegation zone.

State of play

Local reporting agrees the match finished 1-1 and Hampshire and Isle of Wight Constabulary said five people were arrested. The force confirmed a Section 34 dispersal order covered the 12-hour matchday period and that a Section 60AA identity-removal order was in force, and police released arrest details showing some suspects bailed and others held in custody as enquiries continue. Chief Superintendent Simon Dodds described the operation as successful and said he hoped the tactic would give officers another way to respond quickly and deter disruption, an outcome he described as speculative.

Rep Ilhan Omar sprayed with unknown substance during Minneapolis town hall attack. Rep. Ilhan Omar was sprayed with an unknown substance at a town hall in Minneapolis. A 55-year-old man, identified as Anthony Kazmierczak, was immediately arrested by Minneapolis police and charged with third-degree assault. Authorities have not identified the substance; witnesses described a sour, vinegarlike smell and photos from the room showed a light-brown liquid. Omar was not injured, continued to speak and take questions for almost 30 minutes, and later wrote that she was okay.

What happened

Shortly before the incident, Omar had said 'ICE cannot be reformed' and urged that the agency be abolished, telling attendees that DHS Secretary Kristi Noem should resign or face impeachment. During the meeting a man pressed what witnesses described as a syringe and sprayed an unknown substance; video captured someone saying, 'Oh, my God, he sprayed something on her.' Witnesses said the attacker then shouted at Omar, told her to resign and accused her of 'tearing Minnesota apart' before security removed him. Reporting noted his Facebook page contained images of President Trump.

According to police, the man, identified as 55-year-old Anthony Kazmierczak, was immediately arrested by MPD officers, booked into county jail and charged with third-degree assault. The U.S. Capitol Police said the case 'will be met with swift justice' and that they were working with federal partners to pursue the most serious charges possible.

Reactions and opinions

Minneapolis Mayor Jacob Frey condemned the attack, saying, 'Violence and intimidation have no place in Minneapolis.' Representative Nancy Mace said she was 'deeply disturbed' and that no elected official should face physical attacks. Former President Donald Trump publicly questioned the account and accused Omar, saying, 'No. I don't think about her. I think she's a fraud. I really don't think about that. She probably had herself sprayed, knowing her.'

The Capitol Police report recorded 14,938 investigations into concerning statements, behaviors and communications in 2025, a sharp rise from roughly 9,474 the year before; reporting and analysts have treated the Omar incident as part of this broader surge in threats against lawmakers. The attack also came amid heightened tensions in Minnesota after federal immigration operations and two recent fatal shootings by federal officers, reporting noted.

State of play

Reporting and police statements agree that Rep. Ilhan Omar was sprayed with an unknown substance at a Minneapolis town hall and that a 55-year-old man, Anthony Kazmierczak, was arrested and charged soon after. Authorities say the substance has not been identified and forensics processed the scene, and the U.S. Capitol Police said investigators would work with federal partners to seek the most serious charges possible. Competing political interpretations followed: former President Donald Trump alleged the incident may have been staged, Representative Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez speculated it was connected to repeated public attacks on Omar, and the motive remains unknown as authorities work to test evidence and move the case forward.

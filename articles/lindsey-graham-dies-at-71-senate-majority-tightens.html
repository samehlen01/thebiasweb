Lindsey Graham died Saturday night at 71, just two days after his birthday. His office said he died after a brief and sudden illness, and the D.C. medical examiner later said a preliminary examination found an aortic dissection caused by arteriosclerotic cardiovascular disease. Graham had just returned from Ukraine after meeting President Volodymyr Zelenskyy.

51 seats
Republicans' majority in the 100-member Senate
After Graham's death.

Graham's death reduced Republicans' majority in the 100-member Senate to 51 seats. The margin is even tighter because Mitch McConnell remains absent. That narrow edge makes it harder to move Trump's agenda and to manage the chamber's day-to-day business.

Early life and career

He grew up in Central, South Carolina, helped run his family business, became the first person in his household to attend college, and later became guardian to his sister. His life changed while he was studying at the University of South Carolina, when both of his parents died within 15 months of each other.

If it wasn't for my family, friends … and faith, I would not be sitting here today.
— Lindsey Graham, senator

He also served as an Air Force lawyer. He stayed in the Air Force Reserve through much of his congressional career, retiring as a colonel after more than three decades of service. Graham spent more than three decades in Congress, chaired the Budget Committee and led the Judiciary Committee.

From critic to ally

Graham's relationship with Trump shifted from criticism to close alliance after private meetings at the White House and a golfing relationship. The shift was a sharp reversal from the years when he was among Trump's most persistent Republican critics.

Foreign policy and unfinished work

Graham was a hawkish advocate for Ukraine and Russia sanctions, and he supported Israel and a tougher approach to Iran. Just before he died, he announced that he had reached agreement with the Trump administration to move forward on a Russia sanctions package targeting buyers of Russian oil and natural gas.

As Russia intensifies its slaughter of civilians, it is imperative that the legislative and executive branches work together to create tools to exact a heavy price on those who buy Russian oil and natural gas, fueling the Putin war machine.
— Lindsey Graham, senator

Graham's hawkish foreign-policy stance set him apart in the Senate on Ukraine, Russia sanctions, Israel and Iran.

Reactions across party lines

Democratic Sen. Richard Blumenthal said Graham had been over the moon about the sanctions bill and wanted it passed as a tribute. He said Graham's death should be a reason to move the measure forward rather than delay it. He also said he hoped Trump's White House might become more receptive to Graham's nonisolationist foreign-policy view.

In Kyiv, President Volodymyr Zelenskyy called Graham a defender of freedom and the values that make the world safer. Ruslan Stefanchuk described him as a steadfast friend whose support was principled and resolute.

Among Republicans, Tim Scott said Graham was irreplaceable. He credited him with putting faith, family and South Carolina first. Senate Republican leader John Thune called him a strong advocate for the United States and for freedom-loving countries.

He was like a member of the family to me. It's very tough actually.
— Donald Trump, president

Trump said he spoke with Graham in the 7 p.m. hour on Saturday and recalled the senator sounding a little tired after the trip from Ukraine. He also said the two men talked frequently. Trump described Graham as a worker and called him one of the greatest people and senators and a true American patriot.

Separately, Laura Loomer and Tony Seruga quickly pushed claims that Russia, Israel or Iran might somehow be involved. They suggested poisoning and a cover-up, but those posts were speculation rather than evidence.

Replacement race

South Carolina law requires a temporary replacement after Graham's death and a special primary election, and McMaster may appoint the replacement before the special primary.

Trump also said he already had somebody in mind for the race, but he declined to name the person because, he said, it was too soon.

Republicans who want the seat can begin filing on July 21. The primary is set for Aug. 11. A possible runoff would follow on Aug. 25, putting a fast special election in motion for the seat.

Russian reporting emphasised that Moscow opened a criminal case against Graham and placed him on a wanted list after his remarks about Russian deaths; coverage also recalled his calls for "hell sanctions" and punitive 500% tariffs on countries importing Russian oil.

South Asia
Sources
News18 India, Hindustan Times, Dawn Pakistan
I can't die now. I still need to do the Russia sanctions, get Iran sorted out, and do Israeli-Saudi normalization.
— Lindsey Graham, senator

South Asian outlets reported that hours before he died he declined immediate medical attention and said he would wait until his scheduled Meet the Press appearance; they relayed the quoted line about unfinished work on Russia sanctions, Iran and Israeli‑Saudi normalization.

They also reported that Donald Trump publicly recommended Nordone — who has never held elected office — as a temporary Senate replacement and that Graham had adopted his sister so she could receive military benefits; Pakistani coverage added that the Sanctioning Russia Act had White House agreement and 85 Senate co‑sponsors, warning his absence could slow sanctions and Ukraine security aid.

North America
Sources
CBC Canada, Globe and Mail

Canadian reporting said Gov. Henry McMaster appointed Nordone to serve the remainder of Graham’s term, making her the first woman to represent South Carolina in the Senate, and outlined her background as an optician and a state agency employee with a master's degree in rehabilitation counselling.

They highlighted immediate political consequences:

  • A scramble among South Carolina’s conservative hopefuls for the rare open Senate seat.
  • A slimmer Republican majority (with McConnell out) that could complicate passing defence bills, Russia sanctions, confirmations and government‑funding talks.

Outlets framed the appointment as both a symbolic first for the state and a development that tightens Senate arithmetic.

United Kingdom
Sources
The New Arab

UK coverage emphasised fresh foreign‑policy projects Graham was advancing: plans to travel to Saudi Arabia and Israel this autumn to test momentum for a normalization push — discussions he had taken up with Trump and senior envoys — and urging the president to consider a short but overwhelming military operation to reopen the Strait of Hormuz if diplomacy failed.

Oceania
Sources
The Guardian Australia

Australian reporting said around 20 federal agents from the FBI and other agencies attended Graham’s Washington residence and that investigators were probing his death "out of an abundance of caution"; it also recorded the president saying he did not see "a lot of evil" in the death and that the FBI might be "wasting their time" investigating conspiracy theories.

Latin America
Sources
El Comercio Peru

Peruvian reporting noted Graham had visited Ukraine at least ten times since Russia's 2022 invasion and had pushed the 2025 U.S.‑Ukraine critical‑minerals agreement; analysts and Ukrainian officials told outlets his death removed a direct intermediary to Trump and a key legislative actor for Kyiv.

Western Europe
Sources
Der Standard Austria

Austrian coverage resurfaced a 2016 campaign quip in which he joked his unmarried sister could serve as First Lady.

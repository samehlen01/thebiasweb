What happened

Local resort officials said a British man was killed after an avalanche swept an off‑piste area above La Plagne in the French Alps. He was thought to be in his 50s, according to the resort. Resort statements said he had been skiing off‑piste when the slide occurred. The resort said he had been skiing alongside a group when the avalanche struck.

Rescue operation and timeline

Rescue crews said they received an avalanche alert at about 13:57 local time on Sunday and went straight to the site. The operation involved around 52 people, reports said, including medics, ski‑school instructors and a helicopter‑deployed piste dog. After roughly 50 minutes of searching, crews located him buried under about 2.5 metres of snow.

Equipment and official responses

The resort's statement said the skier had not been carrying an avalanche transceiver and was not accompanied by a professional instructor. La Plagne said its thoughts were with the man's family. A Foreign Office spokesperson said it is supporting the family and is in contact with relevant authorities in France. The resort's website also tells off‑piste visitors to read the Avalanche Risk Bulletin and to carry an avalanche‑victim detector.

Reporting differences

Coverage varied in wording when describing the size of the search team: the Guardian wrote of 'a team of more than 50 people', while the Independent referred to 'a team of around 52 people'.

State of play

Resort statements and reporting agree that a British man died in an off‑piste avalanche at La Plagne. Rescue teams found him after about 50 minutes of searching and recovered him from under roughly 2.5 metres of snow, the resort reported.

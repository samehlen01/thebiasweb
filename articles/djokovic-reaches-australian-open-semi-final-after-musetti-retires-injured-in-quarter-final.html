Djokovic reaches Australian Open semi-final after Musetti retires injured. Lorenzo Musetti retired from their quarter-final with an upper right leg problem while leading 6-4, 6-3, 1-3.

6-4, 6-3, 1-3
Score when Musetti retired (Musetti leading)
Quarter-final at Melbourne Park.

The Guardian argued Musetti's withdrawal denied him a shot at his first hard-court Grand Slam semi-final and a potential career-high ranking.

How the match unfolded

Al Jazeera reported Djokovic made 18 unforced errors in the first set and 32 overall before Musetti retired. Djokovic himself said he had 'probably 40 errors' in the match. He was treated for blisters and finished with his right foot heavily taped.

I had four winners in the first two games and no unforced errors and then the rest of the match I had another four winners and probably 40 errors. That's what Lorenzo does to you, makes you play when you think the point is finished.
— Novak Djokovic

Djokovic said the error total reflected how Musetti's variety forced him to keep playing beyond points he thought were finished.

Records and route to the semis

Before the match Djokovic had a 9-1 head-to-head record against Musetti; the retirement took that all-time margin to 10-1.

With the result Djokovic moved to sole ownership of most singles wins at Melbourne Park with his 103rd and extended his record to a 54th Grand Slam semifinal. He had reached the quarter-finals earlier by walkover when Jakub Mensik withdrew.

Reporting varies on how many sets Djokovic played to reach the semis: Forbes counted nine sets, while the BBC recorded 11 sets.

Reactions and views

Several outlets — Al Jazeera, Forbes and The Guardian — described Djokovic as 'extremely lucky' to progress after the retirement. John McEnroe said Djokovic 'snatched victory from the jaws of defeat'. Djokovic said a question suggesting he was 'chasing' rivals was 'a little bit disrespectful'. He added that he did not feel he was chasing and that he was 'creating my own history'.

What comes next

Defending champion Jannik Sinner beat Ben Shelton 6-3, 6-4, 6-4 to set up a semi-final with Djokovic. The progression kept Djokovic's bid for a record 25th major alive, multiple outlets said.

Outlook and speculation

The BBC speculated Djokovic could become the oldest men's Grand Slam champion in the Open era if he stays fit. BBC analysis said that to move ahead of Margaret Court he would need to limit time on court, keep points short and hope for good fortune. Djokovic said 'I know that if I'm feeling well and the body is holding on and I'm playing well, then I always have a chance'.

State of play

Reports confirm Djokovic advances to the Australian Open semi-finals after Musetti retired with an upper-right leg problem while leading 6-4, 6-3, 1-3, and that Jannik Sinner will be his semi-final opponent. The Guardian argued Musetti lost the chance at a first hard-court Grand Slam semi-final and a potential career-high ranking, and multiple outlets noted Djokovic's progression keeps alive his bid for a 25th major. Press reports differ on Djokovic's unforced-error totals and on whether he had played nine or 11 sets to reach the semis, commentators ranged from calling the outcome 'extremely lucky' to Djokovic rejecting a 'chasing' narrative, and analysts speculated about his fitness and match-time strategy.

Australia
Sources
The Guardian Australia, 7News Australia, ABC Australia
I feel personally that I know my body, and I’m sure that it’s a tear.
— Lorenzo Musetti, Italian player

Australian reporting emphasised Musetti's own suspicion that the problem was a tear and described the withdrawal as historically unusual — reported as the first Open Era retirement from a Grand Slam quarter-final or later after a player had won the first two sets.

Coverage also recalled Musetti's earlier major retirements (the 2021 French Open and the 2025 Roland‑Garros semi-final) and highlighted Jannik Sinner's run: the reports noted he is the fifth player in the Open Era to reach six consecutive Grand Slam semi-finals, was on a 20‑match winning streak and had won 23 consecutive matches at the Australian Open.

Swiss reporting noted Djokovic became only the third player (after Jimmy Connors and Roger Federer) to reach 1,400 matches at ATP Tour level; it added he had won only two smaller tournaments in 2025 (Geneva and Athens) and entered the event ranked No. 4.

Austrian coverage specified Musetti's problem as adductor-related and recorded the quarter-final's duration at about 2 hours and 8 minutes.

Spanish outlets flagged Djokovic's age (38 years and 255 days), making him the second-oldest man in the Open Era to reach the Australian Open semis after Ken Rosewall, and described Musetti limping off accompanied by his agent and reportedly leaving in tears.

Apple reports record iPhone sales and strong growth forecast. Apple said iPhone revenue surged 23% year over year to $85.27 billion, marking its best iPhone quarter on record. The company also reported total fiscal first-quarter revenue of $143.76 billion, a 16% year-over-year increase.

Results and outlook

Apple guided March-quarter revenue to grow 13%–16% year over year, putting its revenue outlook between $107.76 billion and $110.62 billion and projecting a companywide gross margin of 48%–49%. Finance chief Kevan Parekh told analysts that the guidance 'comprehends' Apple’s best estimates of constrained iPhone supply, which the company said means sales could have been higher if supply had been unconstrained. Apple executives said the constraints were driven by limited availability of the advanced nodes used to build Apple’s SoCs and warned that rising memory prices would be 'a bit more of an impact' on March-quarter gross margin.

Apple CEO Tim Cook said iPhone demand 'exceeded our expectations', called recent iPhone sales 'simply staggering', and described the company as being in 'supply chase mode' and 'currently constrained'. Apple said supply constraints were limiting iPhone production and that sales could have been higher if it had secured more chips.

The constraints that we have are driven by the availability of the advanced nodes that our SoCs are produced on, and at this time, we're seeing less flexibility in supply chain than normal,
— Tim Cook, Apple CEO

Cook tied the shortage to advanced-node SoC production and said it reduced the company's supply flexibility during the quarter. Executives framed the quarter as demand-led but supply-limited, and some analysts pointed to the interplay of constrained component supply and rising memory costs as an explanation for cautious guidance.

Sales by product and region

Apple reported Services revenue of $30.01 billion and said service gross margin expanded 150 basis points to 76.5%. Services growth moderated slightly from prior periods but remains a central, higher-margin pillar of the business.

Wearables and accessories revenue fell roughly 3%, Mac sales were down just over 7%, and Wearables, Home & Accessories revenue missed expectations at $11.49 billion. That weakness in peripheral hardware contrasted with the strength in iPhone sales this quarter.

Greater China revenue jumped 38% year over year to $25.53 billion, marking Apple's best iPhone quarter in the region. The result in China far exceeded some visible estimates and was a key contributor to the companywide topline beat.

AI, M&A and capital allocation

Apple announced a partnership with Google's Alphabet to use Gemini as the basis for future Apple foundation models and for Siri improvements, though Cook declined to provide further details. RTE reported Apple acquired AI startup Q.ai for $1.6 billion. Apple said it plans to spend $16 billion in the coming fiscal year on retail and infrastructure. The company reported free cash flow of $51.5 billion for the quarter and repurchased $24.7 billion of stock.

Market reaction

Market reaction differed by trading window: RTE and CNBC reported Apple shares rose about 3.5% in extended trading after the results, while the BBC reported the stock fell 10% during Thursday trading, its largest single-day drop since 2020.

Analysts' reading and speculation

Josh Lowitz (analyst) said many users had been holding phones for years and were 'due' for upgrades, helping drive iPhone sales. Dan Ives (analyst) said he expected the iPhone 17 cycle to sustain an upgrade wave for the next 12 to 18 months. Lowitz and CIRP said Apple had streamlined its lineup and that Pro and Pro Max models accounted for around 52% of U.S. iPhone sales in the quarter.

By contrast, eMarketer analyst Jacob Bourne cautioned that Apple's ability to remain on top of the smartphone market is 'perhaps more uncertain than ever'. He also said rising DRAM and memory prices will pressure hardware margins in coming quarters and that strong Services momentum therefore matters more. Anna MacDonald, an investment manager, said investors are wondering what Apple will do next on AI because the company has 'hung back' compared with other big tech names.

Several analysts speculated that future iPhone growth 'all comes down to AI'; Dan Ives said customers have been in a 'waiting game' and that Apple must use the Google Gemini partnership to deliver meaningful Siri upgrades to sustain momentum. Ives and others suggested that if Apple leverages the Google partnership to deliver meaningful Siri/AI improvements, it could materially extend the iPhone upgrade cycle. Separately, AppleInsider (reported by CNET) speculated that an iPhone 18 lineup with C1/C1X modem hardware could make the 'Limit Precise Location' feature available to more devices.

Software and accessories updates

Apple released iOS 26.3 documentation noting a 'Limit Precise Location' setting that is supported only on specific devices and a short list of carriers. AppleInsider (reported by CNET) speculated that an iPhone 18 lineup with C1/C1X modem hardware could expand availability of that setting. Apple also released an updated AirTag 2 with a new U2 chip, about 50 percent longer Precision Finding range, and unchanged pricing of $29 for one or $99 for a pack of four.

What’s clear — and what’s next

Apple confirmed an all-time iPhone revenue record of $85.27 billion. The company also reported total first-quarter revenue of $143.76 billion and guided March-quarter revenue to grow 13%–16% year over year. Finance chief Kevan Parekh told analysts the guidance 'comprehends' the company's best estimates of constrained iPhone supply, and Tim Cook tied the constraint to limited advanced-node SoC availability. Apple announced a Google Gemini partnership and RTE reported a $1.6 billion Q.ai acquisition; analysts speculated those moves will be watched closely as investors judge whether AI can sustain an upgrade cycle and as Apple reports March-quarter results.

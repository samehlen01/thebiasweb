Jury sent out in trial over alleged murder of Blackburn man Paul Scott. A jury at Preston Crown Court was sent out to begin deliberations after a 13-day hearing and, on the thirteenth day, returned unanimous guilty verdicts that Liam Donlin, Joanne Maxwell and Amie Clegg were convicted of murdering Mr Scott. The three were remanded into custody and are due to be sentenced on March 20.

Prosecution case and evidence

Prosecutors told jurors the attack had been planned and motivated by revenge, alleging the trio had conspired together and pointing to recorded threats and messages. Trial evidence said all three collected kitchen knives from their home before they travelled to Blackburn in Maxwell's car. Prosecutors relied on film and phone material and said that Amie Clegg filmed the incident on her phone intending to upload the footage to Snapchat. Police recovered two knives in the sink at Mr Scott's address, one stained with blood, and a third was recovered from Maxwell's car. Forensic testing identified blood consistent with Mr Scott's on Liam Donlin's hands.

Prosecutors described how the fatal blow was delivered: they said Donlin charged through the front door wielding a kitchen knife and stabbed Mr Scott in the heart at the doorstep. Investigators later recovered two knives from the kitchen sink at the address — one showing bloodstains — and a third blade inside Maxwell's car, while testing linked blood on Donlin's hands to Mr Scott.

A Home Office post-mortem concluded that the wound penetrated into Mr Scott's heart. Emergency responders were called to his Peridot Close address on June 22, 2025, and he was pronounced dead at the scene.

Defence accounts and arguments

Defence teams offered alternative explanations. Richard English KC, representing Donlin, accepted his client caused Mr Scott's death but said the stabbing was not intended and that Donlin had gone to threaten and intimidate rather than to kill. Jaime Hamilton KC, for Maxwell, pointed to footage she said showed Maxwell asking 'what have you just done?' and holding car keys with no image of a knife. Clegg's barrister told jurors she phoned 999 and attempted to help, that she lied to the call handler in panic, and that mere presence at the scene does not establish guilt.

A trio of evil attacked, killed and filmed Paul’s last moments in the dead of night on his own doorstep.
— Family of Paul Scott

The family's statement called the defendants 'a trio of evil' and said they had filmed Paul’s last moments, a charge the prosecution used to underline its account of a calculated, revenge-driven assault.

Our thoughts remain with Paul’s family and loved ones following the conviction today.
— Lancashire Police

All three were arrested at the scene. Maxwell and Clegg provided prepared statements denying any intention to kill, while Donlin did not give a formal account. Clegg later phoned 999 after the stabbing, gave false names and told the operator that Paul was already dead. Donlin had pleaded guilty to manslaughter at an earlier hearing and Maxwell pleaded guilty to manslaughter on day two of the trial; Clegg denied the offences until the jury returned guilty verdicts. The defendants faced charges of murder, manslaughter and possession of a bladed article; Donlin admitted possession and Maxwell and Clegg were found guilty of possession.

Donlin, 25, and Clegg, 22, are half-siblings; Maxwell, 44, is their mother, and Mr Scott was Clegg's cousin.

Timeline and speculation

What started as a gathering of family and friends in September 2024 spiralled into months of tension, threats, and confrontations, ultimately culminating in the fatal attack on Paul Scott in June 2025, a timeline sketched by the Lancashire Telegraph and presented as possible background rather than proven motive.

At the heart of the trial was a dispute over intent. The prosecution's case framed the killing as a planned revenge attack, while defence counsel said the outcome flowed from intimidation, bravado and panic. Prosecutor Richard Littler outlined the conspiracy allegation in court; Richard English KC told jurors Donlin had not intended to kill, Jaime Hamilton KC pointed to Maxwell's shocked reaction on film and Clegg's barrister said panicked lies and attempts to help should not be equated with guilt.

Where things stand

Across reporting and the court record there is agreement that Paul Scott was fatally stabbed at his Peridot Close home on June 22, 2025, a Home Office post-mortem found the wound passed through a rib and entered the heart, and a Preston Crown Court jury found Liam Donlin, Joanne Maxwell and Amie Clegg guilty. The prosecution says the assault was planned and motivated by revenge, while defence counsel maintained the deaths were unintended and grew from threats, panic and shock. The defendants have been remanded and are due to be sentenced on March 20, and some reporting has speculated that tensions from a September 2024 family gathering helped set the scene for the fatal confrontation.

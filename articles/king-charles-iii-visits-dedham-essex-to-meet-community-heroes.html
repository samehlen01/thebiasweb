King Charles III and Queen Camilla will visit Dedham, Essex on Thursday, February 5, to meet community heroes and walk through the village.

Visit plan and local preparations

The royal couple will stop at The Sun Inn, which was included last year in The Good Food Guide's list of the 100 best pubs, and will meet community groups such as Next Chapter, the Dedham Art Society and the Dedham Vale Vineyard.

Traffic, parking and shuttle arrangements

Colchester Council warned that Mill Pond and Mill Lane car parks will be closed to new arrivals, and that the High Street, Royal Square, Brook Street, Mill Lane and Southfields Road will be closed or subject to no-parking restrictions while the royal party is in the village.

A Chelmsford Weekly News notice said the Mill Pond and Mill Lane car parks would close to new arrivals at 2pm on Wednesday, February 4.

A Chelmsford Weekly News bulletin said that from 7pm no parking would be allowed in Dedham's High Street, Royal Square, Brook Street, Mill Lane and Southfields Road.

The Colchester Gazette published timings saying those same streets would be closed between 8am and 3pm on the day of the visit.

The notices agree on restrictions but give different hours for various measures, a discrepancy readers should note when planning to attend.

8.30am to 10.30am outbound; 12.30pm to 3.30pm return
Free limited shuttle from Colchester Park and Ride
Shuttle boards at Colchester Park and Ride (off the A12); outbound service 8.30am to 10.30am and returns from Dedham 12.30pm to 3.30pm; Park and Ride charges still apply.

Colchester Council is encouraging people not to travel to Dedham by car, says the event is primarily intended for local residents and warns that vehicles left on restricted roads once the parking controls come into force will be moved.

Local reaction and context

It is a tremendous honour to welcome Their Majesties back to our city. Their visit in 2023 was a moment of great pride for our communities, and we are delighted that Colchester continues to hold a special place in their hearts. We look forward to greeting them again in Dedham and showcasing the spirit, heritage, and warmth that define our city.
— Councillor Michael Lilley, Mayor of Colchester

The visit is the royal couple's first return to the area since March 2023, when they visited Colchester to celebrate it being granted city status.

Locals and visitors spoke warmly about Dedham. Ms Paine said 'It's lovely, beautiful. It's a very quiet, English, country village.' Mr Sturgess added, 'It's quintessentially English, isn't it?' Visitors who had eaten at The Sun Inn told reporters they 'had a very nice meal' and praised its reputation and building, and a Colchester Gazette reporter summed up the village as 'practically faultless' with residents who are 'friendly, welcoming, and polite.'

Local reporting predicts that the centre of Dedham will be very busy on the day of the visit.

One Colchester Gazette writer speculated that a separate published claim that Dedham is 54 minutes from central London may be an overstatement.

The weekend before the Dedham visit, the King surprised participants at a Sandringham parkrun.

The King, who has been receiving cancer treatment as an outpatient since February 2024, told the public late last year that his care would be reduced in 2026.

The Colchester Gazette said its reporters will provide in-depth coverage of the visit and its promotions team will hand out newspapers and celebratory flags to crowds lining the streets.

Estate agents say a three-bedroom house on Dedham High Street will cost about £875,000.

State of play

Across council notices and local reporting, it is confirmed that King Charles III and Queen Camilla will be in Dedham on Thursday, February 5, and that parking and road restrictions will operate. Colchester Council is urging people not to travel by car and has organised a limited free shuttle from the Park and Ride, and it warns that vehicles left in restricted areas may be moved. Local authorities and outlets have said the event is primarily intended for residents, but published notices vary about the precise timing of car-park closures, no-parking windows and street closures, so the exact hours differ by source. Reporters recorded warm local reaction to the visit, and background reporting has noted the King's outpatient cancer treatment since February 2024 and his announcement that his care would be reduced in 2026. Organisers say the Gazette will be on hand to cover the day and hand out celebratory flags.

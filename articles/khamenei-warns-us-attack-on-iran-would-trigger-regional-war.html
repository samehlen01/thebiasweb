Ayatollah Ali Khamenei warned an American attack would spark a regional war. He also accused the United States of seeking to seize Iran's oil, natural gas and other mineral resources.

The Americans must be aware that if they wage a war this time, it will be a regional war,
— Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, Supreme Leader

Khamenei made the remarks in Tehran as tensions with Washington rose. The United States has deployed the aircraft carrier USS Abraham Lincoln and other warships to the region. Iran had planned a live-fire military drill for Sunday and Monday in the strategic Strait of Hormuz.

Ship counts and reporting differences

Reporting on the U.S. presence varied. One account said the US Navy currently has six destroyers, one aircraft carrier, and three littoral combat ships in the region, while other reports put the total at about 10 warships in and around the Middle East.

Casualty figures contested

3,117
Iranian state media total deaths
State media said 2,427 were civilians and security forces and labelled the rest 'terrorists'.

Independent monitors and U.S.-based rights agencies give much higher totals. The U.S.-based Human Rights Activists News Agency said it had verified 6,713 deaths, most of them demonstrators. It also said authorities had detained at least 49,500 people. Other US-based activists said more than 6,300 people were killed and said they were investigating roughly 17,000 reported deaths; one report cited 17,091.

Calls have grown inside Iran and among exiles for an independent, verifiable accounting of the deaths. Mohsen Borhani said citizens should be able to upload names and information so a verification site can confirm and publish details for each announced name.

Diplomacy and talks

President Donald Trump said Tehran was 'seriously talking' with Washington, saying, 'But they are talking to us, seriously talking to us.' He added, 'Of course he is going to say that.'

Iranian officials offered mixed signals about negotiations. Ali Larijani said 'Contrary to the hype of the contrived media war, structural arrangements for negotiations are progressing.' At the same time, there is no public sign of any direct talks with the United States, something Khamenei has repeatedly ruled out. A senior U.S. official told CBS it is unclear whether the U.S. will participate in a planned diplomatic meeting in Turkey.

Warnings, military posture and the IRGC

U.S. Central Command warned Iran against any 'unsafe and unprofessional behaviour' near its forces and against threatening American warships or aircraft. Iran's defence minister, General Amir Hatami, said the country's forces were at a high level of military and defensive readiness and warned that any mistake by an enemy would endanger the security of the region and Israel.

The Guard controls Iran's ballistic missile arsenal, has extensive economic interests, and answers only to Khamenei. Iran has in the past threatened to close the Strait of Hormuz if attacked.

Iranian officials said the Islamic Republic has not sought war and that a broader conflict would harm both Iran and the United States and the wider region.

EU moves, Tehran's response and economic pressure

The European Union added another layer when EU foreign policy chief Kaja Kallas announced the bloc's decision to designate the IRGC as a terrorist organisation. Iran's parliament responded by declaring European militaries 'terrorist groups', a step Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf described as Europeans 'shooting themselves in the foot' by following Washington. Analysts say that while largely symbolic, the EU designation adds to economic pressure on Iran.

Domestic incidents and human costs

Local authorities said a blast at a building in the southern port city of Bandar Abbas killed one person and injured 14, and that an explosion in Ahvaz killed at least four people; local officials blamed both on gas leaks.

Allegations, analysis and stakes

Iranian officials have accused various actors of trying to pull the United States into a conflict. Abbas Araghchi alleged 'It is obvious to us that there are certain elements, certain parties, that want to drag President Trump into this war for their own benefits and I think President Trump is wise enough to make the correct decision.' Editorial commentary in The Independent cautioned that 'A misstep by America in the Middle East could be catastrophic, so it needs to seek a resolution through negotiation.' President Trump also said 'India is coming in, and they’re going to be buying Venezuelan oil as opposed to buying it from Iran.'

Rights groups and analysts have warned that sedition charges in Iran can carry the death penalty, renewing fears that detainees could face mass executions, a development some officials in Washington have described as a red line.

State of play

Across outlets, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei delivered a public warning that an American strike would risk a wider regional war. The United States has increased naval forces nearby. Counting of deaths from recent protests is sharply disputed: Iranian state media reported 3,117 dead, saying 2,427 were civilians and security forces, while the U.S.-based Human Rights Activists News Agency said it had verified 6,713 deaths, and other activists cited more than 6,300 and investigations into roughly 17,000 reported deaths. Ali Larijani said 'Contrary to the hype of the contrived media war, structural arrangements for negotiations are progressing', while there is no public sign of any direct talks with the United States and Khamenei has repeatedly ruled them out. It is unclear whether the U.S. will participate in a planned diplomatic meeting in Turkey. Near-term developments to watch are Iran's planned live-fire drill in the Strait of Hormuz and continuing international pressure after the EU listing. Rights groups warn sedition charges carry the death penalty, and it remains unclear whether President Trump will use force.

Rachel Duffy announces death of her mother days after winning The Traitors. In an Instagram post Duffy said her family were heartbroken and asked for privacy as they grieve, and the message came after she and fellow contestant Stephen Libby were named joint winners and split the show's £95,750 prize pot.

We are heartbroken to share the passing of our beautiful wee mummy. We kindly ask for privacy as our family grieves during this difficult time.
— Rachel Duffy (Instagram)

The Instagram announcement echoed comments Duffy had made while promoting the show about her mother, Anne, who Duffy said was diagnosed with Parkinson's at 47 and later developed dementia.

Win and prize

£95,750
Total prize pot
Split equally: £47,875 each

Duffy and Stephen Libby were declared joint winners, taking home £47,875 each; Libby later said the money had not yet hit his account and that contestants do not receive prize payments until after the show, a timing detail that some reporting argued meant Duffy was unable to use winnings to take her mother on the holiday she had hoped for. She also became the first female Traitor to win the show and the first person from Northern Ireland to do so.

Family and illness

Duffy told outlets she wanted to use any prize money to create memories with her mother and with her children and had hoped to take Anne on a dream holiday; she added that her mother's condition had deteriorated since the series was recorded in May, making long trips impractical.

Dementia UK response

Dementia UK offered condolences, thanked Duffy for speaking candidly about dementia and published a helpline number, and the charity urged families affected by dementia to contact its specialist support.

Audience numbers

The BBC later said the live final peaked at 9.6 million viewers, while another BBC report described the fourth series as having attracted more than 12 million viewers; those figures appear in different pieces of reporting and relate to different audience measures.

Reactions and speculation

Duffy said sharing her story resonated with many viewers and carers, and a BBC-England analysis credited the series' drama, Stephen's meme-friendly expressions and Faraaz's insight with helping to drive interest. A celebrity agent told the Daily Mail that Duffy is 'a superstar' who has been the standout contestant and will be inundated with representation offers, and Jonathan Shalit described her as the 'perfect commercial package' who will attract brands. Shalit also speculated she could earn well into seven figures next year, and a Daily Mail piece suggested her income is set to rocket and she may become a millionaire and the richest Traitor yet; those latter points are speculative projections by industry figures and reporting.

State of play

Reporting confirms that Rachel Duffy announced her mother's death and asked for privacy, and that she and Stephen Libby were named joint winners who split a £95,750 prize. Dementia UK offered condolences and urged families to use its specialist helpline, while Libby said the prize money had not yet hit his account and some outlets analysed that delay as preventing Duffy from funding a planned holiday for her mother. Audience figures differ across reports (9.6 million peak vs more than 12 million across the series), and industry figures have predicted strong commercial interest in Duffy — predictions which remain speculative as payments and offers are still pending.

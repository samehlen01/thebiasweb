Two men died after two children got into difficulty in the water at Seaton Carew beach at about 3.45pm on July 12, prompting a police response, Cleveland Police said. Two men went into the sea to help the children. The children were safe and later taken to hospital for checks.

Police also said two other men entered the water to assist and got out safely. One of those men, Davey Short, later said he and another man helped a child to shore, adding that he had acted on instinct as a father.

The first two men were brought out by the RNLI, given immediate medical treatment and later pronounced dead.

North East Ambulance Service said it dispatched three ambulance crews, a clinical team leader and a duty officer, and also sent three crews from its Hazardous Area Response Team (HART). It requested support from the Great North Air Ambulance Service, which attended by road.

Police, the RNLI, the Coastguard, ambulance crews and air support attended the incident. The two men were taken to North Tees Hospital for further treatment.

Cleveland Police said the deaths are not being treated as suspicious and a file will be prepared for the coroner. An inquest is likely to take place.

Tributes and reaction

The deaths prompted shock, and Jonathan Brash called the scene a heartbreaking tragedy and said the families were in the town's thoughts, adding that no words could lessen the pain.

Council leader Graham Harrison said the men's courage, selflessness and sacrifice should be remembered.

Social media users posted tributes online and described them as brave and heroic.

Water safety warnings

  • Superintendent Glen Ward said warm weather can make open water look inviting, but the risks remain serious and people should not enter it in hot weather.
  • Former lifeguard Gareth Parker said warning signs are often ignored and rip tides and undercurrents are underestimated.
  • Amy Waites said people should not assume any stretch of water is safe.

RNLI spokesperson Ross Macleod said people should remember Float to Live, which asks swimmers to let themselves float, recover their breathing and then seek help or try to reach safety, and that it gives people a chance to regain control and make a better decision once they are in the water.

Lifeguard cover and review

Hartlepool Borough Council said lifeguards currently patrol Seaton Carew only during school holidays, with the season due to start on July 18. It had asked for a full review of the current water safety arrangements at Seaton Carew and across the borough's coastline.

Rebecca Ringwood launched a petition after the tragedy to restore lifeguard services beyond school holidays, and it gathered more than 1,500 signatures.

Rebecca Ringwood said holiday-only lifeguard cover leaves the beach under-protected during busy periods.

Councillor Sue Little said the beach is busy in warm weather well before the school-holiday patrol season begins, and that trained lifeguards on peak days could prevent incidents, offer immediate help and reduce emergency callouts.

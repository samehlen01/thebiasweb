Spain beat France 2-0 to reach the 2026 World Cup final. Mikel Oyarzabal scored from the penalty spot in the 22nd minute after Lucas Digne fouled Lamine Yamal. France finished the first half without a shot on target and with just two attempts overall. Pedro Porro added Spain's second goal in the 58th minute as Spain controlled the semifinal.

Spain's control

37 matches

record-equalling mark for a European side

The numbers underline Spain's control, and they have also conceded only one goal at the tournament.

Spain are the best team in the world.
— Luis de la Fuente

De la Fuente credited the structure that has carried them this far and said it held up under pressure in the semifinal. Pedro Porro framed the win as a collective effort, saying Spain needed possession to blunt France's strengths rather than chase the game on the break.

Reaching the final is a dream come true.
— Mikel Oyarzabal

Oyarzabal said Spain want one more step.

France's frustration

France were below their usual level.
— Didier Deschamps

Deschamps said France made technical mistakes and did not carry enough threat in attack. The defeat also ended Kylian Mbappe and France's bid to reach a third straight World Cup final. Deschamps also questioned Ivan Barton's handling of the game, saying the referee was not up to the demands of a World Cup semifinal. Rayan Cherki said France lost to themselves. The defeat was not down to the referee or to Spain, Cherki said.

Chris Sutton described Spain's display as a football lesson. Patrick Vieira said Spain were the better side and France never really turned up. Gary Neville pointed to Porro and Cucurella at full-back and said Lamine Yamal delivered in the biggest moments. David James argued that Spain's possession can frustrate even gifted attacks like France's.

What comes next

Spain will face England or Argentina in Sunday's final at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey. Spain can still repeat the 2010 European Championship-World Cup double with a win on Sunday. If England beat Argentina, Spain and England would meet again in a final that would echo Euro 2024. France will now go into Saturday's third-place play-off, and Deschamps will step down after the tournament.

Justin Gaethje defeats Paddy Pimblett to win interim lightweight title at UFC 324. Gaethje won by unanimous decision in Las Vegas after a five-round main event; judges scored the fight 48-47, 49-46 and 49-46. Paddy Pimblett suffered his first UFC loss, snapping a nine-fight winning run and dropping to a 23-4-0 career record.

Fight details

The victory gave Gaethje his second career interim lightweight championship. Multiple sources forecast that the result sets up an undisputed title fight with Ilia Topuria when Topuria returns, and Topuria is expected to be back at some point in 2026.

Accounts differ on how many times Pimblett hit the canvas: some reports said Pimblett was dropped twice during the fight. Other outlets, including the Daily Mail, recorded three knockdowns.

Pimblett was taken to hospital after the fight for additional checks. UFC president Dana White said referee Mark Smith had to be carried from the arena with an ACL injury. White also praised Pimblett's toughness and durability and said in an official statement that 'it's tough to tell' whether the punishment he absorbed will affect him long term.

Pimblett's response and mental-health plea

You live and you learn. I’m 31. I’ll be back better. It’s as simple as that. You haven’t seen the last of me.
— Paddy Pimblett

After the decision Pimblett said he had wanted to leave with the belt and vowed to come back better. He also used his platform to urge men to speak up about mental health, saying two men he knew had died by suicide in recent months. Pimblett said 'I thought 48-47 was a fair scorecard.'

Reactions

Gaethje described Pimblett as very dangerous and said he had to steal his momentum and his confidence. Joe Rogan told Pimblett his performance in defeat was like a victory. Ilia Topuria taunted Pimblett online, saying he had lost the biggest payday of his life and that 'you're screwed no matter what.' A section of the Vegas crowd reacted with dismay after the Silva–Namajunas decision and believed Namajunas had done enough to win.

Other results

In the co-main event, Sean O'Malley defeated Song Yadong by unanimous decision (29-28x3). Waldo Cortes-Acosta knocked out Derrick Lewis at 3:14 of the second round. Natalia Silva beat Rose Namajunas by unanimous decision in a potential flyweight title eliminator. Jean Silva defeated England's Arnold Allen in a slugfest that was decided in the third round on two of three official scorecards.

Dominick Cruz was announced as the first member of the UFC's 2026 Hall of Fame Modern Era Wing.

State of play

Reports agree that Justin Gaethje defeated Paddy Pimblett by unanimous decision at UFC 324; judges scored the bout 48-47, 49-46 and 49-46; Pimblett was taken to hospital after the fight; and referee Mark Smith suffered an ACL injury. Multiple outlets and UFC figures forecast that Gaethje's interim win sets up an undisputed title fight with Ilia Topuria when Topuria returns. They also suggested Natalia Silva's victory may put her in position for a shot at Valentina Shevchenko. Dana White and other officials praised Pimblett's toughness but said they could not tell whether the punishment he absorbed will have long-term effects, and Pimblett urged men to speak up about mental health. Reports differ on whether Pimblett was dropped twice or knocked down three times, and the immediate next steps are medical checks and negotiations around the lightweight title picture.

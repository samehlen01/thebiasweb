The blaze began on Saturday, July 11, shortly after 8.30pm, and kept burning into Monday.

Smoke spread across Greater Manchester and nearby areas. It was not confined to Saddleworth, and people reported haze and the smell of smoke over significant distances.

A major incident has been declared over a moorland fire near Dovestone Reservoir in Saddleworth. Greater Manchester Police said the 20-year-old woman had been arrested in Oldham and remained in custody on suspicion of arson.

  • Residents were told to stay indoors where possible and keep windows and doors closed because of concerns about health and air quality.
  • Road closures and no-access restrictions remained in place near the reservoir. Greater Manchester Fire and Rescue Service also asked people to avoid the Dovestone area so emergency services could keep access routes clear and work safely.

Emergency response

Greater Manchester Fire and Rescue Service declared a major incident and brought in extra resources to contain the blaze. Firefighters first used water and grass beaters. Once a water supply was established, they moved into an offensive firefighting operation using hose lines.

The response drew in five fire engines from Mossley, Stalybridge, Hyde, Bolton North and Littleborough, alongside specialist wildfire units and officers.

Police investigation

Greater Manchester Police said: "We are aware of social media posts circulating regarding this incident and would like to stress that this is a live police investigation."

Officers were following lines of enquiry and told people to stop speculating.

Cause unconfirmed

The cause had not been confirmed.

Eyewitnesses reported loud bangs. Social-media clips appeared to show fireworks being let off before the fire took hold. Those reports prompted speculation that careless fireworks may have started the blaze.

Local reaction

Jake Frater said the fire was destroying wildlife and habitats, putting people at risk and harming the countryside. He said the damage should not become another annual pattern. He said smoke was affecting residents' health and air quality.

Residents described the fireworks as reckless, dangerous and stupid. Oldham councillor Keiron Lawrence said he had been in contact with residents throughout the day and that some of those responsible had already been identified and the material was being reviewed. "Those responsible, some I can confirm have been identified, should face the full consequences available, including substantial fines where appropriate."

Andy Holian said firefighters were working tirelessly in extremely challenging conditions. He said they deserved gratitude and respect.

Crews were expected to keep tackling the blaze over the coming days, with restrictions still in place near the reservoir.

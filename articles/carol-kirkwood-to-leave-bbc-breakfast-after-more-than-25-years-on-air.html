Carol Kirkwood to leave BBC Breakfast after more than 25 years — Carol Kirkwood announced on BBC Breakfast that she will leave the BBC in April after more than 25 years. She said she wants to spend more time with her husband, Steve, and to travel.

I've loved my job, I've loved working at the BBC, but I love my husband more than my job.
— Carol Kirkwood

The announcement followed her regular morning forecast, when the 63-year-old sat on the Breakfast sofa to break the news.

Discrepancies in reporting

Coverage differs on basic details. BBC - Entertainment & Arts described the departure as coming 'after more than 25 years', while the Daily Record's headline framed it as 'quits after 28 years' and raised questions about her pay. The Daily Record also reported that she first joined the BBC in 1993 as a freelance reporter, while other outlets and the BBC say she trained with The Weather Channel and the Met Office before joining the BBC Weather Centre in 1998.

Background

Kirkwood received meteorological training at The Weather Channel and with the Met Office before joining the BBC Weather Centre in 1998.

She has been the main weather presenter on BBC Breakfast since 2010. She has repeatedly been named best TV weather presenter at the TV and Radio Industries Club awards, and in 2015 she competed on Strictly Come Dancing, reaching week eight.

She will be greatly missed by teams across the BBC. We wish her all the best for the future.
— Jonathan Munro

Jonathan Munro said Kirkwood had made an exceptional contribution to the corporation and that she will be greatly missed by teams across the BBC.

Reactions and speculation

I will miss her in the mornings. But I cannot wait for Carol's next chapter.
— Sally Nugent

Eamonn Holmes wrote 'Missing you already, Carol xxx' in response to Kirkwood's announcement. The broadcaster has not yet confirmed who will take over her role. Some coverage has raised questions about Kirkwood's pay; one outlet estimated her salary is under £150,000.

What happens next

Across the initial coverage, the confirmed facts are that Carol Kirkwood announced she will leave the BBC in April and that she said she wants to spend more time with her husband and to travel; the BBC's Jonathan Munro paid tribute and said she will be greatly missed. Outlets disagree on tenure and origins of her BBC career — the Daily Record framed her exit as 'after 28 years' and reported she first joined the BBC in 1993 as a freelance reporter, while other reporting points to a 1998 move into the BBC Weather Centre after meteorological training. The broadcaster has not yet confirmed who will take over her role and publicised salary figures remain speculative, with one outlet estimating her salary is under £150,000.

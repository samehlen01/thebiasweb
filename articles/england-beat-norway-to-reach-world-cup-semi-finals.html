England beat Norway 2-1 after extra time in Miami to reach the World Cup semi-finals. Jude Bellingham's two goals took his tournament tally to six. It is only the fourth time they have reached a World Cup semi-final, and England's last two semi-final appearances ended in defeat to West Germany in 1990 and Croatia in 2018.

First, Jude Bellingham levelled England at the end of the first half of extra time. He scored again at 93 minutes, taking his tournament tally to six. Then came the detail that it was the second time the Stourbridge-born player scored in a World Cup quarter-final. Harry Kane is also on six, leaving the two of them joint top scorers in the tournament.

The quarter-final kicked off at 5 p.m. ET on July 11 at Miami Stadium and was available on ITVX.

Match reaction

Tuchel said England were lucky, sloppy and made too many technical mistakes. He said the team did not meet his standards because of errors and a lack of speed.

Kane said the side still has another level to find and added that Tuchel wants to see the version of the team that shows up in training, although good opposition makes it hard to show it fully. Bellingham said it had been a tough shift and defended the players against criticism by pointing to Norway's attack and the conditions.

Elsewhere, insiders said there is no issue between Tuchel and Bellingham, and according to Opta, Tuchel's win rate was 80 per cent.

Spider-cam row

FIFA responded with a technical explanation from its Connected Ball sensor.

Before England's goal in minute 45+2 against Norway, the sensor in the Connected Ball showed no peak in the 'heartbeat of the ball' when in the air
— FIFA

FIFA said there was no sign of contact, and therefore no evidence that the ball touched the overhead wire and changed the movement of the ball. Andreas Schjelderup said it would be a scandal if the ball had hit the spider cam. Norway coach Ståle Solbakken said he thought the spider cam hit the ball and called it strange. Daily Star Sport later said fresh footage appeared to show possible contact, although it also said the picture remained unclear. Daily Mail Sport later described the issue as still being argued over.

Off the pitch

Fans in Preston filled pubs and bars to watch and celebrate England's quarter-final win over Norway. People in Oslo and across the UK stayed up late to watch the match. A Worcester venue described the night as frantic, chaotic and electric when Bellingham scored the winner. The Gemini in Dereham said stock was stolen during the England vs Norway match and reported the incident to police.

Kate Kane and Megan Pickford marked England's win on Instagram and said they were ready to head to Atlanta. Prince William congratulated England, calling them a special team and saying the result was never in doubt. John Stones' wife Olivia kissed him after the win, and Maya Jama watched the game before heading to the airport.

Atlanta build-up

Their reward is a Wednesday meeting with Argentina in Atlanta, kicking off at 20:00 BST, live on BBC One and iPlayer. Atlanta police said they are working on an intense security operation for England-Argentina because of the rivalry's history.

Pub owner Skye Stevens expected Wednesday to be another very busy night, with walk-ins and people coming straight from work. Worcester venues were expected to screen the semi-final as well. She also forecast a 3-2 England win over Argentina and a place in the final.

England had already been dealing with fitness and availability issues: Reece James was recovering, Jordan Henderson missed out, Jarell Quansah was suspended after his red card against Mexico, and Declan Rice was ill.
